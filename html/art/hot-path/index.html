A6 · The Hot Path — LMAX on the BEAM · The Art of BCS

The way to remove writer contention
is to not have writers.

Part VI is the turn from refusal to construction. Parts II through V removed the constellation member by member; this chapter builds the thing that takes their place — the matching engine, the hot path where orders meet and fills are made. The pattern it builds on is the one a financial exchange already proved at the limit: the LMAX architecture and its Disruptor. State that prior art fairly, name what transfers to the BEAM and what is dropped, and the core falls out as one bounded ring, one process, one pure function.

The senior continuation of /bcs, whose as-built ring and identity law sit beneath the price-time priority here. Each figure is verbatim from the manuscript (art6.md), its cited sources, or the worked exemplar (exchange.patterns.md and the shipped Exchange.* rungs). The LMAX number is LMAX’s own, named as prior art — not a claim about this exemplar.

§1

State the prior art fairly

art6.md · what this chapter builds on

The prior art is real and well-made, and it is stated first, from its own source. LMAX is a retail financial trading platform whose Business Logic Processor handled, in its published account, six million orders per second on a single thread — running entirely in-memory using event sourcing, surrounded by Disruptors, a concurrency component implementing a network of queues that operate without locks. The Disruptor itself is a pre-allocated, bounded ring buffer: producers add and consumers process, all memory allocated on startup, the ring sized as a power of two so a bit-mask replaces the remainder operation, designed to be cache-friendly and lock-free on modern hardware.

The design’s central conclusion is the one this chapter inherits, and it generalizes past the hardware that produced it: the single biggest limit on a hot path is having multiple writers contend for a value, and the way to remove it is to not have them. That conclusion is the chapter’s starting point — not a claim the runtime invented, but one the runtime realizes by a different mechanism.

One line on provenance, because the course’s discipline depends on it. The six-million figure is LMAX’s own published number for its own platform; it appears here as prior art the chapter translates, not as a measurement of this exemplar. The figures that are this exemplar’s — the codec cost, the reconcile, the gate counts — are committed transcripts the three dives quote, never asserted on this landing.

§2

What transfers, and what is dropped

art6.md · the chapter’s discipline

The translation to the BEAM keeps the principles and drops the machinery, and saying which is which is the chapter’s discipline. What transfers is the shape; what is dropped is the part the BEAM’s own model makes unnecessary. The seat does not contend, because there is no second writer to contend with — the single-writer principle is a process by construction, not a configuration to enforce.

Transfers whole

The shape of the path

A bounded ring at the door for back-pressure, a single writer for the business logic, state held in memory and rebuilt by replaying events, and the mechanical-sympathy habit of pre-allocating rather than allocating on the path. The principle is LMAX’s.

the architecture carries over intact

Dropped, deliberately

The lock-free machinery

Busy-spin waiting, shared-memory sequence barriers, multi-consumer coordination — the Disruptor’s machinery exists to share one ring across several JVM threads without locks. On the BEAM none of it is needed, because the seat is a process, not a pinned thread.

the JVM-thread machinery is not required

The realization

A process, not a thread

One process owns the book, is its only mutator, and waits without spinning because the scheduler preempts it. EchoCache.Ring is therefore an admission buffer that provides back-pressure, not an inter-thread channel.

the principle is LMAX’s; the realization is the runtime’s

Read together: the shape transfers, the machinery drops, and the single-writer seat is a process by construction — so the contention the Disruptor engineers away is not engineered away here, it is structurally absent.

§3

Latency by construction, and the built core

art6.md · the admission-reconcile invariant

The hot path holds a microsecond budget not by tuning but by shape: one bounded buffer, one process, and one pure function, so there is no lock to contend, no queue to swell unobserved, and no shared state to coordinate. Overload is a typed answer at the door rather than a latency discovered downstream — the admission-reconcile invariant, where accepted equals applied plus counted drops, so nothing is lost silently and overload is a number. The core that realizes this is built and gated: Exchange.Gateway parses untrusted input once into typed commands carrying branded ids and integer money, Exchange.OrderBook is a pure price-time ladder, and Exchange.Decider emits fills as facts.

Price-time priority falls out of the branded id’s byte order, so the core needs no clock to break ties: the id is minted at admission, mint order is sequence, and the same order holds in every store by the byte-sort theorem. Select two resting orders at one price to read which fills first, and why the answer is the mint order rather than a separate timestamp.

readout · Select a question above. On the bid ladder, price ranks first and mint order breaks the tie within a price — the order minted first fills first. The rank is the branded id’s own byte order, so the book needs no separate clock to rank two equal-priced orders.
§4

Strategies on the same pattern

art6.md · the layer above the engine

The layer above the engine is the engine’s own pattern applied again: a strategy is a pure decider that emits intents, arranged as a four-stage signal–sizing–risk–execution pipeline, with risk expressed as gating deciders and the kill switch realized as the lane verbs the bus already has. Route a strategy’s intents through its own bus group and the committed pause, resume, and limit controls become its kill switch, throttle, and slow restart — no new mechanism, the bus’s own.

Because a strategy is the same decide-then-evolve shape as the matching core, its evaluation is not a separate simulator but the one implementation replayed over recorded input. A backtest is the system run again, not a second system written to approximate it — so a strategy that passes its backtest and a strategy that runs in production are the same code on the same fold, which is the property the pattern was chosen to buy.
