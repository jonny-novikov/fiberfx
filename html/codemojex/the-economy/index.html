Codemojex · chapter C5

The economy and the bank.

Money is real, so it lives on the floor: three currencies each in its own lane, every balance change a database transaction, and a settlement that pays the same on a re-run. The candid economy — fees stated, the pool stated up front, the conversion rate fixed and public.

planned this chapter ships as a gated stub — the thesis and the dive map below; its authoring rung deepens it

§1

Three currencies

dive · planned

Keys pay in paid rooms and are bought through the KeyShop; clips pay in free rooms and carry no value; diamonds are prizes, convertible to keys at a fixed ten to one. The paid and free paths never cross.

§2

The transactional wallet

dive · planned

A balance change locks the player row with SELECT … FOR UPDATE, checks the non-negative invariant, writes the balance, and inserts the paired TXN row — all or nothing. Wallet.buy_in (cm.5) is the two-sided form: the player debit and the atomic pool credit in one transaction, exactly-once by a partial unique index. The row lock serializes only same-player mutations, so the field never funnels through one process.

§3

The bank, the pool, and the rake

dive · planned

Today the pool lives on the game’s own prize_pool: platform-seeded for an ordinary room, funded by the field’s buy-ins for a Golden Room, paid out at settlement. The BNK escrow as a first-class entity and the published platform rake are forward (cm.9+) — the ruled default is transparent margin levers, never undisclosed house players.

Grounding — wallet.ex, economy.ex, ledger.ex, the player/transaction schemas, the economy decision surface (§8), and the wallet · economy · golden-economy · golden-tournament stories.
