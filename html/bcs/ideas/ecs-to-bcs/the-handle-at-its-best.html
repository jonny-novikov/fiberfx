B1.4 · dive — the handle at its best

The handle, at its best.

Before the chapter buries the index-handle, it states the modern discipline at full strength: centralized systems own their allocations, only index-handles cross to the outside world, and spare bits carry a generation counter. Read with Part I’s eyes, that is two of the law’s three clauses discovered independently, inside one process.

Source: content/bcs1.4.md · What, quoting West 2007 and Weissflog 2018. The argument is sympathetic by design.

§1

The discipline

content/bcs1.4.md · What

Weissflog’s 2018 treatment states the modern form cleanly: move memory management into centralized systems, “the systems being the sole owner of their memory allocations”; group same-typed items into arrays whose base pointers are system-private; and let only index-handles cross to the outside world, with the handle’s spare bits carrying a generation pattern — a per-slot counter after the November 2018 update — so a stale handle is usually caught when the slot is reused.

Read with Part I’s eyes, the discipline is clause one (systems own their state) and clause two (only identities cross) discovered independently — enforced by convention inside a single address space. The third clause has no ECS form at all; its absence is the next dive’s subject.

§2

The 2007 confession

content/bcs1.4.md · What

Even the pattern’s founding document records the pressure on clause two. West’s 2007 component article reports that when manager-mediated access cost five percent of CPU, “we allowed the components to store pointers to one another” — the traveling pointer, admitted under frame-rate duress, inside the very article that taught the industry components.

The admission is the load-bearing detail. Convention holds until the frame budget objects; nothing in the address space refuses the crossing. A law enforced by agreement bends exactly where the BCS substrate’s gates do not — the refusals of the PASS 6/6 transcript are the same clauses with the enforcement moved from review into the platform.

§3

The generation counter, exercised

a model of the per-slot check

The model below steps one slot through a fixed event sequence and runs the generation check as a pure function — handle admitted when its generation equals the slot’s. The model gives the slot two generation bits, so its counter wraps after four values; real engines carry more bits, and the same wrap. Detection works at step 4 and fails at step 5 — “isn’t waterproof”, collision detection over a reused slot, probabilistic by construction.

The chapter’s diagnosis lands in the next dive: detection of reuse is the wrong ambition once the minting law makes reuse unrepresentable. A name that is never reissued has no ABA case to catch.
