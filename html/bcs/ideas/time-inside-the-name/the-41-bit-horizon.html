B1.5 · dive — the 41-bit horizon

September 2093, recorded.

The layout grants 41 bits of milliseconds above a 2024-01-01 epoch (1704067200000); 2⁴¹ milliseconds is about 69.7 years, so the timestamp field exhausts in September 2093. The horizon is recorded here rather than discovered later — seventy years is enough runway to make the successor epoch a planned chapter, never an incident.

Source: content/bcs1.5.md · What and Decisions; the epoch and the layout are the contract’s (content/vectors.json: epoch_ms, timestamp_bits).

§1

The arithmetic, stated plainly

content/bcs1.5.md · What

41 bits of milliseconds above 1704067200000. 2⁴¹ milliseconds is about 69.7 years. The field exhausts in September 2093. Nothing here is an estimate of load or a guess about growth — the horizon is a property of the layout, computable on the day the contract was signed, and the contract carries the inputs:

The order theorem of Chapter 1.2 makes the same bits do double duty: because the timestamp rides in the high bits, string order is time order, and every page of this course leans on that. The horizon is the other side of the same grant — a field this useful is a field with an end, and an architecture that quotes its measurements records its expiry date in the same ledger.

§2

The field, consumed

computed from the contract’s constants

Three checkpoints, all from committed sources: the epoch itself, the canonical decode vector’s millisecond (1780512970164 — read from USR0NgWEfAEJfs in vectors.json), and the field’s last millisecond (2⁴¹ − 1 above the epoch). The readout computes milliseconds above the epoch and the share of the 41-bit field consumed — pure BigInt arithmetic over the contract’s constants.

§3

The successor epoch, planned

content/bcs1.5.md · Decisions

A successor epoch is a contract amendment with a wire-version bump and a drain-and-switch lane — the same shape the EchoMQ migration already uses. The decision of record: the horizon is 2093 and recorded; the successor epoch is a planned amendment riding the existing wire-version lane, not an emergency.

That is the whole point of recording the horizon on signing day. An architecture that discovers its timestamp field in a production incident writes the amendment under duress; an architecture that records the date in Part I writes it as a chapter, seventy years early, with the lane already proven by a different migration.
