B3 · The Bus — manuscript Part III

The law, set in motion.

Part I gave every row a name and Part II gave the names a home; Part III puts them in motion. EchoMQ 2.0 is the manuscript's bus — built Valkey-native from the first byte: a fresh emq: keyspace, a version-fenced Lua bundle negotiated before any command runs, and no compatibility ballast from any predecessor wire.

The cargo law is the series' oldest — only identities cross. A job names the entities it concerns; it never carries their rows, and everything the queue does moves fourteen-byte names whose bytes are already chronology. Each module below teaches one written chapter, its committed rung quoted verbatim on the page.

§2

The laws of the part

content/bcs3.md

Part III states six laws and holds every chapter to them. They are the manuscript's, named here so the modules below read as applications of a rule rather than a list of features.

LAW 1

One transition, one script

Every job state change — enqueue, claim, complete, retry, dead-letter — is a single EVALSHA against the versioned bundle. Partial states are unrepresentable: no wire sequence exists that could produce one.

LAW 2

The fence before the first command

The echomq:2.0.0 handshake runs at connect and at every reconnect; a bundle-version mismatch is a typed refusal at boot, never a degraded session. Wrong-kind servers are refused the way wrong-kind ids are.

LAW 3

Jobs are entities

Chapter 3.2 registers JOB under the D-8 bar — jobs are minted, gated, paged, and audited like any kind, and the order theorem gives queue browsing for free because job ids sort by submission.

LAW 4

Park, don't poll

Idle consumers block on keys and cost nothing; readiness arrives as a wake, not a discovered flag. Fairness across groups is constructed — round-robin lanes by assignment — never hashed.

LAW 5

Delivery semantics, named per surface

At-least-once is stated where it holds and made cheap to honor: enqueue is idempotent by job id, and checkpoints survive consumer crashes so a redelivery resumes instead of repeating.

LAW 6

Rivals measured with advantages printed

The closing chapter benchmarks against the transactional-enqueue class. Oban's same-transaction enqueue is a real advantage, and its number appears beside the bus's — a gate that hides the rival's win is decoration.

§3

The floor under the floor

committed outputs, quoted verbatim

Part III is fully written, and every chapter lands with a rung — an executable check and a frozen transcript, committed beside the prose. The course inherits the ethic: a number that is not in a committed output does not appear on a page. The six chapter rungs, the conformance record, and the connector gate on file:

The connector gate ran against live Valkey; the conformance bench measured the bus against Oban 2.18.3 on PostgreSQL 16.14, with the asymmetry stated before the first ratio — the rival's enqueue is durable and transactional, the bus's is volatile by Decision D-2. What the part will not do is stated too: no Streams or PubSub (EMQ 3.0 by Decision D-3), and no cluster routing — the slot function is committed and parked, single-instance Valkey the stated topology.
