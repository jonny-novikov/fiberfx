B8.2 · dive 3 — the double-entry ledger

The stream for unsettled, the ledger for settled.

The platform keeps two records and decides, in the open, which holds which truth. The stream — the journal's log of fills — is the source of truth for unsettled state; the Postgres ledger is the regulated record of settled positions. Settled money posts double-entry, every posting one Ecto.Multi. The compaction that keeps the log small is as-built EchoCache.Journal behavior, quoted from its record; the Trading.Ledger that posts the settled side is a PROPOSED design object.

Source: compact(j) and the writer's price are real Elixir at echo/apps/echo_cache/lib/echo_cache/journal.ex, recorded in bcs_rung_4_4_check.out (PASS 6/6). Trading.Ledger and Exchange.Projection are PROPOSED (docs/trading/) — taught as design, never as a measured number.

§1

Compaction is coverage — the outbox empties, the memory does not

bcs_rung_4_4_check.out · H5 · verbatim

The same coverage predicate that drives replay retires the outbox: an intent is retired when its name carries an applied version at least as new — coverage, not acknowledgment. After the loss drill closed at 50 of 50, all 50 intents are deletable, the applied memory keeps all 50 names, replay finds nothing, and a reopen still remembers. The committed gate:

compact(j) is the retention verb, idempotent and safe to run on a schedule; the outbox stays small, the last word per name persists. Intents are small, coverage is one SQL pass, and an outbox kept under a few thousand rows replays in milliseconds.

§2

The price of memory

bcs_rung_4_4_check.out · H6 · verbatim

The journal's durability is not free, and the record prices it exactly. On prepared-once statements — the single writer's privilege, with bind resetting the statement — the writer's pair is two WAL commits and one cached rowid read at synchronous=NORMAL. The committed gate:

The priced pair is 524 µs (the remembered lane's end-to-end median) against the bare lane's committed 148 µs — the two numbers travel together — and 143 µs is the record-and-mark pair at the writer's edge. At synchronous=NORMAL the WAL issues no per-commit sync — the checkpoint is the sole I/O barrier — which is the journal's speed and its stated power-loss trade. The numbers carry their header: one scheduler, this container's disk, a 29-byte payload; the 3.5 multiple travels better than the 143.

§3

The regulated ledger — PROPOSED

docs/trading/trading.specs.md · design voice

The settled side is the design's Trading.Ledger (status PROPOSED). Double-entry in Postgres: branded id columns under the SQL canon with the domain's reject table as the floor, every posting one Ecto.Multi — a single all-or-nothing transaction, so a fill either posts both entries or neither. The directional decision is named in the spec and revisitable only by editing the spec: the stream (the journal, then milestone-B stream lanes) is the source of truth for unsettled state; the Postgres ledger is the regulated record of settled positions.

Exchange.Projection (also PROPOSED) is the idempotent log consumer that folds the log into Tables — the read path the platform serves positions and balances from. Both modules are taught as design; neither has a rung yet, and so neither claims a latency, throughput, or posting number. The first such number lands when the first rung's harness runs (docs/trading/trading.roadmap.md, TRD.3 and TRD.5).

§4

Why the split, weighed

docs/trading/trading.specs.md · the design's reasoning

The two-store decision is argued, not assumed.

OptionWhat it buysWhy it is not the whole answer
one Postgres ledgerregulated and queryable for everything, settled or notevery unsettled tick becomes a synchronous SQL write on the hot path, where the fold of an in-memory log answers for free
one stream onlyreplayable and fast, no relational dependencya regulated record of settled money wants the SQL canon's constraints, the reject table, and the audit a relational ledger gives
the split (chosen)unsettled where replay is cheap, settled where regulation is requiredthe boundary is named in the spec, decided in the stream's favor for unsettled and Postgres for settled — revisitable only by editing the spec

The design splits the difference along the settlement boundary. The Postgres canon the ledger posts to is the substrate of /redis-patterns and /elixir; the stream lanes the unsettled log moves to are /echomq. The module returns to the capstone at B8.2 · The Log and the Ledger; the strategies that emit intents over this same log are the subject of B8.3 · Strategies as Deciders, a PROPOSED forward module.
