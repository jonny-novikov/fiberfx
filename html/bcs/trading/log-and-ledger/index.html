B8.2 · The Log and the Ledger — the trading corpus, docs/trading/

Two memories: the log, and the ledger.

A trading platform keeps two kinds of memory, and they belong in two places. The stream — the append-only log of every fill — is the source of truth for unsettled state, and recovery from it is replay, not a feature. The ledger — settled money, posted double-entry — is the regulated record of settled positions. The log layer is the as-built EchoCache.Journal under a pluggable EchoCache.Shadow, quoted from its committed record; the regulated Trading.Ledger is a PROPOSED design object.

The journal is real Elixir in the live umbrella (echo/apps/echo_cache/lib/echo_cache/journal.ex), on the committed B4.4 record (content/bcs4.4.md, bcs_rung_4_4_check.out, PASS 6/6) — every journal figure is verbatim from there. The Trading.Ledger / Exchange.Projection modules are the Operator's design (docs/trading/), status PROPOSED: taught as design, never as a measured platform number.

§2

Two memories, one file

bcs_rung_4_4_check.out · the schema

The journal carries two memories, and the schema is the cargo law. The intents table is Richardson's transactional outbox — record the intent before the bus hears it, mark it after. The applied table is the lane's last word per name — the newest version this lane has ever applied, surviving the table, the node, and the bus. The committed gate states it exactly.

MemoryCarriesRole (as-built — bcs_rung_4_4_check.out)
intentsseq, recorded job_id, name_id, version, enqueued flag, recorded_atthe transactional outbox: record before the enqueue, mark after; replay re-enqueues every uncovered intent under its recorded job id
appliedname_id (key), the newest version, its seqthe lane's last word per name; lives in the file, so it survives the table, the node, and the bus; the dedup floor and the retention rule
One journal file per group. Named by the group's branded id; one owner process per journal — the single writer of Chapter 4.3 as a storage discipline. A non-branded group is refused at the door. The bus gets no WAL; the journal, which is not the bus, gets SQLite's. Durability lives in a file the bus has never heard of, which keeps D-2 untouched: the bus stays volatile, recovery stays replay.
§3

The as-built memory — the journal, committed

quoting bcs_rung_4_4_check.out

The module invents nothing under the memory layer. EchoCache.Journal is real Elixir in the live umbrella (echo/apps/echo_cache/lib/echo_cache/journal.ex), one per group, owned by one process. Its committed behavior is the whole six-gate record — the schema, both crash seams, the cross-restart memory, the loss drill, compaction, and the price — quoted verbatim. These are the as-built journal's committed numbers, never the trading platform's own.

The platform's own numbers — the settlement latency, the posting throughput, the fill counts — do not exist yet, and so they appear nowhere on these pages. The first such number lands when the first rung's harness runs (docs/trading/trading.roadmap.md, TRD.3).

§4

The shadow, pluggable — and the regulated ledger, PROPOSED

shadow.ex / litestream.ex · docs/trading/trading.specs.md

The off-box layer is named and pluggable, not built into the journal. EchoCache.Shadow (echo/apps/echo_cache/lib/echo_cache/shadow.ex) is a behaviour — one contract, start_link/1, restore/1 returning {:ok, :restored | :no_replica}, status/1, stop/1 — with two implementations: EchoCache.Litestream (litestream.ex) replicating each per-group journal to S3-compatible object storage and restoring before the journal reopens, and EchoCache.Shadow.Copy snapshotting to a local directory on a development laptop. The supervisor and the restore path never know which one is wired — the same contract either way, and the kind law holds at the shadow's door.

The settled side is the design's Trading.Ledger (PROPOSED, docs/trading/trading.specs.md): settled money posts double-entry in Postgres, branded id columns under the SQL canon, every posting one Ecto.Multi — a single all-or-nothing transaction per posting. The directional decision is named in the spec: the stream is the source of truth for unsettled state, the Postgres ledger the regulated record of settled positions. Exchange.Projection (also PROPOSED) folds the log idempotently into Tables. Both are taught as design; neither has a rung yet, and so neither claims a number.
