The Protocol · the foundation

The substrate the
pillars share

The keys, the record, the scripts, and the immutability that make one wire serve three pillars.

Below the language line is the protocol — the part every pillar and every runtime shares. This chapter teaches it in depth, and it is all real code: the braced emq:{q}: keyspace that EchoMQ.Keyspace builds; the job record hash and its field names; the atomic Lua scripts run by EVALSHA, every key declared; and the immutability and branded-id discipline — the version fence, the 14-byte branded id — that keeps the wire honest. Master this and the Queue, the Bus, and the Cache are three readings of one substrate.
