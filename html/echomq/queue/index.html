The Queue · pillar I

Distribute work
over the wire

Enqueue, claim, complete — one job, one worker, a state machine over four sorted sets.

The Queue is the first pillar: a producer enqueues a job, one worker claims it, runs it, and completes it — and a failure retries with backoff or dies into a morgue. It is all real code. The machine is four sorted sets (pending, active, schedule, dead) where the active-set score is the lease and the row's attempts is the fencing token — no separate lock. Above it sit fair lanes over a rotating ring, a consumer loop that parks instead of polling, bulk batches, the lifecycle controls, and parent/child flows.

The life of a job

A job is one row and a place in one of four sorted sets. Every verb of the Queue moves it between those places. Pick a state to read what it means, the set it lives in, and where it goes next — then take the module that owns those transitions.

One row, four sets: claim leases it, complete retires it, retry reschedules or kills it, and reap recovers the lease a dead worker never returned.

The durable floor

A queue keeps its history in memory until a system decides how much to keep. What it trims is not lost: EchoStore.StreamArchive folds the trimmed segments into the durable EchoStore.Graft floor — CubDB's append-only B-tree, on to Tigris object storage — deep history without resident memory, readable beside the live tail. That floor is taught in full in Echo Persistence.
