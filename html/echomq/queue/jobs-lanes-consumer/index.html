Jobs, lanes & the consumer

The lifecycle is the machine. This module is the people who run it — the producer, the fair worker, and the loop that owns the rhythm.

A job enters the machine through one verb and leaves through another, but between them stand three roles. The producer admits work with one idempotent script. The fair worker claims in turn off a constructed ring, so no identity starves the rest. The loop owns the cadence — a supervised process that parks rather than polls, costing the wire nothing while it waits. Pick a role below to read the real surface it owns, then take the three dives.

Three roles over one wire

A job's working life runs through three hands: the producer that admits it, the worker that claims it fairly, and the loop that decides when to look. Each is a real surface in echo/apps/echo_mq. Pick a role to read the verb it owns, what it touches, and the verdict it returns.

Three roles, three verbs, one wire: admit once, claim in turn, and look only when there is reason to.

Pattern & implementation

Where Redis Patterns Applied teaches the move — a reliable queue that admits work once and hands it to exactly one consumer — this module is where the move lands, with fairness and a parking loop built on top.

the pattern (Redis Patterns Applied)

A reliable queue admits each item once and delivers it to exactly one worker, which claims, runs, and acknowledges — blocking for the next item rather than busy-polling. Reliable queues teaches it.

the implementation (echo_mq)

EchoMQ.Jobs.enqueue/4 admits idempotently; EchoMQ.Lanes.claim/3 serves identities in turn off a rotating ring; EchoMQ.Consumer runs the loop and parks on the wake key with BLPOP.
