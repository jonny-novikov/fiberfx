The Proof · module 02

How it reports
on itself

Push the events as work happens; pull the state whenever you ask — and never change the work either way.

A proven system still owes you one answer: how is it doing right now — and it has to answer without being asked to change. The bus answers two ways, mirror images of each other. Push (EchoMQ.Meter): as work happens the lifecycle emits [:emq, …] events the standard Elixir :telemetry way — attach a handler and throughput, latency, and failures flow to it, at zero cost when telemetry is absent. Pull (EchoMQ.Metrics): a pure-read plane answers counts, a job's state, throughput, and the rate-gate off the as-built structures — every verb observes, none mutates. One side observes by emitting; the other answers by reading. It is all real code.

Push and pull, one lifecycle

Both reports watch the same thing — a job moving through its lifecycle — from opposite sides. The meter sits on the work and emits an event at each transition. The read plane sits to one side and, when asked, counts how many ids are in each state right now. Neither moves a job. Step a job through its life to see both reports update.

Two reports of one life: the meter says it as it happens, the read plane answers it when asked — and the work is untouched by either.
