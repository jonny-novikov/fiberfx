The consumer group

Many readers, one log — each entry handed to one, acknowledged, and resumable across a crash.

Module 02's read/6 is a stateless replay: every reader sees the whole log from the head. A consumer group is the other kind of read — the reliable, distributed one. Many cooperating readers share one stream, each entry is delivered to one of them, acknowledged with XACK, and resumable. The group remembers each consumer's un-acked backlog — the PEL — so a reader that crashes resumes where it left off without replaying what it already handled. The promise in one line: read >, ack, resume. And the cost, named up front: at-least-once, not exactly-once. It is all real code.

Each entry once, resumable

A group is one stream read by many cooperating consumers, where the group keeps each consumer's PEL — the list of entries delivered to it but not yet acked. That one piece of bookkeeping is what makes the read reliable: an entry goes to one consumer, the consumer settles it, and what it does not ack survives to be redelivered. Pick a state below — the steady fan-out, a consumer crash that leaves a PEL, the peer reclaim — and read what the group does, the Valkey verb beneath it, and the guarantee.

The PEL is the whole trick: an entry goes to one consumer, and what is not acked is not lost — it waits, redeliverable, until someone settles it. Read >, ack, resume.

The rest of the pillar

The consumer group is the reliable read. It sits on the stream log (module 02), the writer that mints the entries it consumes. Beyond it, time-travel reads the log by a mint instant, and retention bounds it — folding what it trims into the durable floor. The events channel beside them is the present-tense surface the log makes durable.
