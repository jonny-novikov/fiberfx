The Bus · pillar II

Broadcast signals
over the wire

Fan a message out to everyone, then keep an ordered, replayable log of what was said.

The Bus is the second pillar, and it is the mirror of the Queue. Where the Queue hands one job to one worker, the Bus does the opposite: it broadcasts to everyone, and it keeps an append-only log many readers replay at their own pace. Two surfaces, one wire. Events (EchoMQ.Events) are fire-and-forget pub/sub — a consumer reacts to work as it happens without polling. The stream log (EchoMQ.Stream) is a retained, replayable log ordered by mint — consumed by groups at-least-once, read by a mint instant for time-travel, bounded by retention, and what it trims is folded into the durable floor. The thread through both is the branded id. It is all real code.

The two surfaces, one wire

The Bus is one wire read two ways. Events broadcast a signal to whoever is listening, now and gone. The stream is the same idea made durable: an append-only log, ordered the way the store is ordered — by the branded id — that survives, replays, and folds to disk. Pick a verb to read what it does, the Valkey command beneath it, and the guarantee it makes.

One wire: events say it once, the stream remembers — appended in mint order, replayed by a group, read by an instant, trimmed by policy, and what is trimmed is folded, not lost.

The durable floor

A log that only grows is a leak, so retention is a policy — and what a stream trims is not lost. EchoStore.StreamArchive folds the trimmed segments into the durable EchoStore.Graft floor — CubDB's append-only B-tree, on to Tigris object storage — at a reserved page range ordered by the branded EVT id, readable beside the live tail through a watermark merge-read. Deep history without resident memory. That floor is taught in full in Echo Persistence.
