Retention, and what it keeps

Bound the live log so it cannot leak — and fold what you trim to disk, so the past is kept, not lost.

An append-only log appends forever, and memory is finite, so the live log has to be bounded. Retention is how: EchoMQ.Stream.trim/4 caps it by length (MAXLEN) or by age (MINID). The trap it refuses is the one that makes retention dangerous elsewhere — coupling a safety property (bounded memory) to a liveness fact (a consumer is up). So the trim cadence lives on its own beat. And the whole point of this module: what is trimmed is not lost. Before a record leaves the live stream it is folded into the durable Graft floor — committed, kept, and readable beside the live tail. A stream bounded in memory but deep in history. This is the Bus's door to persistence. It is all real code.

A log, a trim, a fold

Three phases. The live log grows as records are appended in mint order. A trim bounds it — XTRIM removes the records outside the window. But the trim is not the first thing to touch a record on its way out: a fold writes it to the durable floor first, ordered by the branded id, where it stays queryable. Step the three phases below to read what each does, the verb beneath it, and the guarantee it makes.

Retention is a policy, not a default — and the trimmed past folds to the floor, kept and queryable beside the present. Bounded in memory, deep in history.

The dives

Three dives: the trim and its safe default, the fold that keeps what the trim removes, and the durability dial the archive sits on. Each grounds in the real echo/apps/echo_mq and echo/apps/echo_store code — EchoMQ.Stream.trim/4, EchoMQ.StreamRetention, EchoStore.StreamArchive. The trim and the fold are issued direct — there is no Lua here.

The durable floor

A log that only grows is a leak, so retention is a policy — and what a stream trims is not lost. EchoStore.StreamArchive folds the trimmed segments into the durable EchoStore.Graft floor — CubDB's append-only B-tree, on to Tigris object storage — at a reserved page range ordered by the branded EVT id, readable beside the live tail through a watermark merge-read. Deep history without resident memory. That floor is taught in full in Echo Persistence, and narrated in the manuscript at The Branded Component System · the persistence floor.

The rest of the pillar

Retention closes the Bus's reading of the retained log. The stream log writes it; the consumer group reads the live tail forward; time-travel reads it by a mint instant. This module bounds it and folds what it trims to disk — and that fold is the pillar's door to the persistence floor, where the whole durability story is told.
