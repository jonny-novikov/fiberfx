M0.3 · Overview

The mesh.

The menu offered four strategies; this dive states which one EchoMesh is built on and what it takes to hold it together. The architecture is three moves — segment the trade across surfaces, place those surfaces on an owned stack, and make the infrastructure beneath the stack transparent. The segmentation coheres only because a single branded identity crosses every surface, and the transparency comes from running the same program on a laptop and on a fleet of ephemeral machines.

A dive of M0 · Overview, the orientation chapter of EchoMesh, In Depth. No measurement here: this dive describes an architecture, and the figures that test it live in the chapters it points to — the seam at M5, the stack at M6, the transparent runtime at M7. The infrastructure claims rest on five cited primary sources.

Proposed · not shipped

This dive leans heavily on the mesh. The pieces it places are real and shipped — the BEAM, Postgres with Ecto.Multi, Tigris, Fly Machines, the FLAME pattern; EchoMQ and EchoCache are real components. Their composition into the segmented mesh described here is the proposed design, taught in forward voice. The synthesis that separates the shipped substrate from the proposed whole is M8.2.

§1

The architecture, in three moves

mesh.0.3.md · EchoMesh's stance

EchoMesh is a segmented system held whole by one identity, placed on an owned stack, on infrastructure that disappears into the design. The previous dive established segmentation as the dominant strategy; this dive states the three moves that turn it into an architecture, and marks the line between what ships and what is proposed.

Move one · M5

Segment the trade, sewn by one identity

Assign a different trade to each surface, and keep the whole coherent with a single 14-byte branded identity minted once and carried unchanged. A fill matched on a consistency-first book is the same entity in the cache, the stream, the store, and the worker.

the brand is the seam · one entity, many trades

Move two · M6

Place the surfaces on an owned stack

Each surface sits where its trade belongs — the BEAM and a single writer for consistency, EchoCache and the EchoMQ bus and streams for availability, Tigris for globally replicated retention, Postgres with Ecto.Multi for the ledger that blocks rather than diverges.

the whole guarantees neither · each part what it should

Move three · M7

Make the infrastructure transparent

The FLAME pattern lets one program treat its whole self as elastic — the same code on a laptop and across a fleet of subsecond-launch Fly Machines, with placement by the branded id over a consistent-hashing ring that computes the same owner on one node or a thousand.

scale is a property of the design · not a target to operate

Why the order matters. Segmentation is the design and the brand is what keeps it from fragmenting; the owned stack is where each segment's trade is honoured; transparent infrastructure is what lets the whole run unchanged from one machine to many. The three moves are taken in their own chapters — M5, M6, and M7 — and the synthesis that separates the shipped substrate from the proposed whole is M8.

§2

The seam — one identity across every trade

mesh.0.3.md · the branded seam

Segmentation assigns a different trade to each surface, which raises the question the strategy lives or dies on: if a fill is matched on a consistency-first book, cached on an availability-first feed, logged to a stream, retained in an object store, and priced by a worker, what makes those five touches the same fill. The answer is a single 14-byte branded identity, minted once and carried unchanged across every surface.

Because the id is stable and self-describing, no surface needs to agree with any other on storage or timing to agree on identity; the consistency-first core and the availability-first periphery can choose opposite trades and still name one entity. This is the seam that lets a segmented system stay whole — taught behind the /bcs door and built at M5.3. Segmentation is the design; the brand is what keeps it from fragmenting.

The branded id names state rather than carrying it. A surface holds whatever representation its trade calls for — a row in the ledger, an entry in the cache, a record in the stream, an object in the store — and the id is the one thing common to all of them. That is what lets the menu's fourth strategy work without dissolving: the system is many trades, and the brand is the single fact every trade shares.

§3

The stack, placed on the trade

mesh.0.3.md · the owned stack

EchoMesh assigns its surfaces across an owned stack, each piece sitting where its trade belongs rather than on rented infrastructure chosen for it. The signature figure above places the five surfaces on the spectrum; the line between what stays consistent and what stays available is drawn piece by piece in M6. Two surfaces are consistency-first and three are availability-first.

The engine runs on Elixir and the BEAM, whose built-in distribution, supervision, and message-passing process model carry the coordinator, log, broker, and orchestrator as properties of the language rather than services to wire together. The bus and the per-instrument stream log run on EchoMQ; the near-cache, the placement ring, and the journal on EchoCache — the availability-first delivery path and its log. Deep retention runs on Tigris, a globally distributed, S3-compatible object store that writes close to a region and replicates close to requesters, an availability-first tier with no configuration to operate. The regulated ledger runs on Postgres, with Ecto.Multi composing several writes into one all-or-nothing transaction that rolls back as a unit — the consistency-first surface that blocks rather than diverges.

Real components, proposed composition. EchoMQ and EchoCache are real components, and the BEAM, Postgres with Ecto.Multi, and Tigris are shipped technologies. What is proposed is their assignment to these trades as one stack — the placement, not the pieces. M6 draws each line in full.

§4

Transparent infrastructure

mesh.0.3.md · FLAME, Fly Machines, M7

The usual case for renting a global constellation rests on how hard it is to run your own; EchoMesh's third move removes that difficulty rather than paying to avoid it. The FLAME pattern lets one program treat its whole self as elastic — modular work runs on short-lived infrastructure with no rewrite and no bespoke runtime, on any cloud with an instance API. The grain underneath is the Fly Machine, a subsecond-launch virtual machine that starts and stops on demand, its lifecycle and region controlled through a REST API.

The same code runs on a developer's laptop and, deployed across a fleet of those machines, scales by changing almost nothing. Placement is by the branded id over a consistent-hashing ring, which computes the same owner on one node or a thousand, so capacity is a property of the design rather than a deployment to operate. The loss worth naming is that this assumes an instance API to call and a workload that partitions cleanly by identity; within those bounds, infrastructure becomes transparent — the same EchoMesh, whatever it runs on. This is M7.

Within its bounds, and no further. The transparent-infrastructure claim holds within those two assumptions, and is not a promise of linear scaling. M7 takes the claim apart — the same-code property, the ephemeral machine, and placeless placement — one dive each.

§5

Real and proposed, candidly

mesh.0.3.md · the line, marked · M8.2

The pieces of this dive exist and are in use: the BEAM, Postgres with Ecto.Multi, Tigris, Fly Machines, and the FLAME pattern are shipped technologies, and EchoMQ and EchoCache are real components. What this course introduces is their composition into the segmented mesh described here — the assignment of surfaces to trades, the single branded seam across all of them, and the transparent-infrastructure deployment as one coherent system. That composition is the proposal, and the synthesis that separates the shipped substrate from the proposed whole is M8.2.

This dive states the architecture; it does not build it. The identity contract is summarized, not specified — the specification is the /bcs course and M5.3. The stack is placed, not benchmarked — the measurements are in M6. Each surface's exact trade is asserted here and defended in its own chapter.
