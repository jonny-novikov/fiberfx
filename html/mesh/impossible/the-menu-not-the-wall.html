M1.3 · Doing the Impossible

The menu, not the wall.

The theorem forbids one small region of the design space, and “two of three” mistakes that boundary for a wall. The useful reading is a menu of defensible answers and a question asked surface by surface — what does this subsystem do during a partition, and how does it recover after.

No measurement here: this dive is about design stances, not numbers. Two primary sources carry it (Brewer 2012, Gilbert & Lynch 2012) — the retrospective that calls “2 of 3” misleading and frames partition handling as detect, partition mode, recover, and the catalogue of four coping strategies. The strategies and the framing are external and settled; where EchoMesh places each is named as design and built in later chapters. The dominant answer — segmentation — is the heart of the course at M5.

Proposed · not shipped

The menu and its framing are external results. EchoMesh's assignment — which surface takes which strategy, and the branded seam that keeps a segmented system whole — is the course's proposal, built on shipped technology and composed into the mesh; the substrate-versus-proposal line is drawn in full at M8.2.

§1

Why “two of three” misleads

mesh.1.3.md · Brewer 2012

The crude reading invites a system to keep two properties and forfeit the third, and Brewer's retrospective dismantles it on three fronts. The slogan was misleading from the start because it oversimplifies tensions that are continuous and local rather than binary and global.

First

Partitions are rare

There is little reason to give up consistency or availability when the network is healthy. The theorem only constrains behavior during an actual partition — an occasional condition, not a standing one.

forfeit nothing while healthy · an occasional condition

Second

The choice is fine-grained

It is not made once for a whole system but many times over. Different subsystems can choose differently, and the choice can vary by operation, by the specific data, or by the user involved.

many times over · by subsystem, operation, data, user

Third

The properties are continuous

Availability ranges from none to full, consistency comes in many strengths, and even partitions admit nuance — including disagreement within a system about whether one is happening.

not binary · strengths, not switches

What the theorem actually forbids: a single small region — perfect availability and consistency in the presence of partitions — and everything outside it is open to design. Reading the result this way turns it from a wall to scale into a question to answer for each surface.

§2

The real question — behavior under a partition

mesh.1.3.md · Brewer 2012

Replacing the slogan leaves a concrete question, and Brewer gives it the operational shape the emulator above walks: detect the partition, enter a partition mode that either limits the operations allowed — trading availability to protect an invariant — or proceeds while logging enough to repair afterward — trading consistency to stay available — and then recover once communication resumes. Which operations a surface permits during the partition is set by the invariants it is unwilling to break.

Brewer's banking example makes this concrete. The essential operations are deposit, withdraw, and check balance, and the invariant is that the balance stay non-negative. Only withdraw can violate it, so only withdraw needs special treatment under partition, while deposit and balance-check can always proceed. The answer a surface gives is a behavior, not a label — and it is read off the invariant, not chosen in the abstract.

Why this carries the course: every EchoMesh surface answers this same question in its own chapter — the answer a behavior under partition, set by what that surface refuses to break. The menu of answers working systems have settled on is the four coping strategies, next.

§3

The four strategies as the menu

mesh.1.3.md · Gilbert & Lynch 2012

The menu of defensible answers is the four coping strategies Gilbert and Lynch catalogue, each a point on the field the slogan flattened. The first three commit a whole service to one stance; the fourth is different in kind. Each maps to an EchoMesh surface, built in a later chapter.

§4

Segmentation is the dominant answer

mesh.1.3.md · Gilbert & Lynch 2012

The fourth strategy admits what the first three set aside: a real service holds many requirements at once, so it segments itself and assigns each part the trade that part owes its users. The whole guarantees neither strong consistency nor full availability, while each piece guarantees exactly what it should. Gilbert and Lynch name five dimensions to segment along, and most large systems in practice use some blend of them.

The strategy only coheres because one entity stays addressable as it crosses surfaces that have chosen opposite trades — the seam a branded identity supplies and the /bcs course specifies. That is why segmentation is the heart of the course rather than one option among four: it is the frame in which the other three become local decisions rather than global ones.

By data. Different data takes different trades within one service — some records held strongly consistent, others allowed to drift and reconcile. The choice follows what each datum can tolerate going stale.
By operation. Different operations choose differently, as Brewer's banking example shows: a balance-check or deposit can always proceed under partition while a withdraw is restricted, because only withdraw can break the invariant.
By function. Different functions of the system take different stances — a matching core kept consistent, a delivery feed kept available — each a separate subsystem with its own behavior under partition.
By user. The trade can vary by who is involved — some users or accounts served from a strongly consistent path, others from an available one, when their requirements differ.
By hierarchy. The choice can vary along a hierarchy of the data or the system, with different levels taking different trades. Most large systems use some blend of all five rather than one alone.

Hover or focus a dimension to read it; click to pin. Without scripting, all five read in sequence. The five are Gilbert & Lynch's; real designs blend them.

§5

Recovery is half the contract

mesh.1.3.md · Brewer 2012

The menu is not only what a surface does during a partition but how it repairs afterward, and the repair is where the availability-first choice pays its deferred bill. Brewer surveys four ways a system reconciles state once a partition heals, and constraints that are cheap to detect and resolve — such as duplicate identifiers — can be tolerated during the partition and cleaned up on recovery.

Reconcile · pick or merge

When a rule fits

Pick one update and discard the other when loss is acceptable; or merge operations intelligently when the data structure allows. Cheap-to-resolve constraints, like duplicate ids, are tolerated and cleaned up on heal.

last-writer-wins / merge · automatic, no human in the loop

Reconcile · escalate or compensate

When no rule fits

Escalate to a human when no automatic rule applies; or undo committed work with compensating transactions — a saga of smaller steps each reversed by a correcting entry, the way an accounting error is fixed by an offsetting line rather than an erasure.

escalate / compensating saga · a correcting entry, not an erasure

EchoMesh's split falls out of this directly. The consistency-first core blocks rather than diverges, so it has nothing to reconcile; the availability-first periphery answers from local state and logs enough to reconcile on heal. The branded identity that names each entity across surfaces is also what makes the reconciliation tractable, because the same fill is recognizable wherever it was touched.

Real and proposed, candidly: the menu and its framing are Gilbert and Lynch's catalogue and Brewer's retrospective — external and settled. EchoMesh's proposal is the assignment of strategies to surfaces and the branded seam that keeps a segmented system whole; those surfaces run on shipped technology, and their composition into the mesh is the course's proposal, separated from the shipped substrate at M8.2.
