What a glossary entry is

A glossary entry has three faces. The abstract defines the term in one sentence. The Source grounds it in a real book or reference. The framework link points at the /elixir/phoenix chapter where the Portal implements the idea — where the abstraction becomes built code. The entry is the locator, not the lesson.

The list is data-driven from a fixed term set, and it degrades. Without a script every term and its abstract are already on the page, every panel open; the script adds the fold so the list collapses for scanning, and a text filter to find a term. Nothing is stored in the browser. That is the contract of a reference: legible without enhancement, scannable with it.

The glossary is the place to look a concept up and jump to its real code, not the place to learn it first — for that, follow the framework link or the related module.
