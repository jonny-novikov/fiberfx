The crosswalk

Each course idea mapped to the /elixir/phoenix chapter that implements it — the nine F6 rungs as routes you can open.

The crosswalk is where the course's abstractions become routes: lifecycle, routing, ecto, contexts, heex, liveview, pubsub, deployment, live-dashboard — each 200, all under one master invariant. Pick an idea on the right to get its rung and route; the section below groups the rungs into the three shippable milestones.

The crosswalk

A crosswalk row maps a course idea to the F6 rung that realises it, the /elixir/phoenix chapter, and what that rung ships. The nine rungs are verbatim from the F6 phoenix.roadmap.md — three milestones, one shippable capability per rung, all of it over the unchanged Portal facade.

The master invariant spans all nine: the web layer calls only the Portal facade and renders only the closed %Portal.Error{} set.

The three milestones

The nine rungs climb in three milestones, each a shippable product step: ship the catalog, make it live, ship to users. Pick a milestone to light its rungs and read the end-state — the thing a real role can do once the milestone lands.

A roadmap orders the work into shippable milestones — each a product step a real role can use, not a technical layer.

The crosswalk on the Portal

The crosswalk is the F6 phoenix.roadmap.md read as a lookup table. Take "durable persistence": the roadmap line is F6.3, the milestone is "ship the catalog", the spec it points at is the F6.3 triad, and the behaviour the line defines is none — the line orders the work; the spec defines it. Open /elixir/phoenix/ecto and the idea is built code: the Postgres adapter behind the F5 port, the changeset the parse boundary.

# One crosswalk row, from the F6 phoenix.roadmap.md:
#   idea:      durable persistence
#   rung:      F6.3
#   milestone: ship the catalog
#   points at: the F6.3 spec triad  (behaviour defined by the line = none)
#   route:     /elixir/phoenix/ecto  (the idea as built code)

# Re-ordering the rungs never edits a spec — the line carries order, not behaviour.
the principle

A roadmap line orders the work and points at a spec; it defines no behaviour — re-ordering the rungs never edits a spec.
