A3.6 · The roadmap layer

The program roadmap

A chapter roadmap orders rungs. One altitude up, the program roadmap orders whole chapters — the roadmap of roadmaps.

The Portal is one domain core behind one Portal facade, surfaced through several adapters. The web chapter F6 and the Telegram-bot chapter F10 are parallel surfaces over that one facade, each a roadmap of its own, shipping on independent cadences. Switch the planning level on the right to see what each level sequences.

Two levels of planning The three dives References

Order the chapters, not the rungs

A backlog rolls up into a chapter roadmap; a set of chapter roadmaps rolls up into a program roadmap. The unit changes with the altitude. At the program level the planning unit is the chapter, and the artifact is portal.roadmap.md: it sequences whole chapters — F4 store, F5 engine, F6 web, F10 bot, F7–F9 multi-runtime — into four program milestones. At the chapter level the planning unit is the rung, and the artifact is phoenix.roadmap.md: it sequences nine rungs f6.1…f6.9 into three milestones.

The Portal is one domain core behind one Portal facade, surfaced through several adapters. The web (F6) and the bot (F10) are parallel surfaces over that one facade — neither depends on the other for its core loop, so they are sequenced by product priority, not a hard dependency, and they ship on independent cadences. The master invariant threads through every chapter: every surface calls only the facade and renders only the closed error set, so adding a surface adds a roadmap of its own and never changes the core.

the principle

A program of many surfaces stays plannable when each surface's roadmap is independent of the others — sequenced by product priority, not by a hard dependency on a sibling.

on the Portal

“Call only the facade” — the master invariant. The web (F6) and bot (F10) each call only the Portal facade, so each carries a roadmap of its own and ships on its own cadence.

The program roadmap orders chapters; the master invariant is the seam that keeps each surface's roadmap its own.
