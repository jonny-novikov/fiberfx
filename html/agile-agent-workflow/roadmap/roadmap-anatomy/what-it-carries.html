A3.3 · Anatomy of a roadmap.md · dive 1

What it carries

A complete chapter roadmap carries six parts, each answering a different question — and none of them defining behaviour.

Read them in order on the real phoenix.roadmap.md and the shape of the document becomes a template. Pick a rung on the right to watch one roadmap line decompose into its rung, its milestone, the spec it points at — and the behaviour it defines, which is always none.

The six parts, in order

A chapter roadmap is a structured document. phoenix.roadmap.md carries six parts, each a named section, each answering a different question:

delivering
What we are delivering — the destination, verbatim: “The Portal, served to people: a real web application that renders the catalog, lets learners enroll and progress…”.
handoff
Where this starts and ends — the boundary, verbatim: “Start (the F5 handoff).” in, and “End (after F6.9).” out.
architecture
Architecture decision — standard Phoenix on the BEAM, verbatim: “The stack is standard Phoenix, Ecto, and LiveView on the BEAM — the conventional, well-supported path — with no separate frontend application.”
milestones
The delivery arc — three milestones, verbatim: “1 · Ship the catalog”, “2 · Make it live”, “3 · Ship to users”.
the table
Per-rung iterations — the per-iteration table (the A3.3.2 dive): rung · ships · demo · harness · feedback asked.
decisions
Seams & open decisions — the named, unresolved choices (the A3.3.3 dive).

Over all six sits the master invariant, verbatim: “The web layer calls only the Portal facade and renders only the closed %Portal.Error{} set. No controller, LiveView, plug, or template names Portal.Engine, a repo, or GenServer.call.” In the file's own words, “This single rule is what makes the ladder cheap”.

A part answers a question, a line points at a spec

The six parts are the document's anatomy; within the delivery arc, each rung is one line. A rung line does four things and no more: it names the rung, it belongs to a milestone, it points at a spec, and it leaves behaviour to that spec. The hero figure decomposes a single line into those four facets — the fourth, behaviour, always reads none.

The part picker below walks the whole anatomy instead of a single line. Select a part to see its question and a verbatim phrase from the file. The two figures teach different moves: the hero zooms into one line, this one steps across all six parts.

Six parts, six questions, one invariant — and within the arc, each rung line points at a spec and defines no behaviour.

What it carries, on the Portal

On the Portal — the learning platform this course builds — the real artifact is phoenix.roadmap.md, the delivery plan for the web chapter F6. It carries the six parts in order, and within the delivery arc the per-rung table lists nine rungs f6.1 through f6.9. Each row is one line that ships a slice; each points at its f6.N.md spec, where the behaviour is actually defined.

The line is coarse on purpose. “f6.1 — the engine served as a web app” orders the work and names a demo; the contract — the request path, the facade call, the rendered error — lives in the spec the line points at. The roadmap holds order; the spec holds behaviour. That separation is what lets the next dive re-order the rungs without editing a single spec.

# phoenix.roadmap.md — the per-rung table (a line per rung; verbatim cells).
#   | Rung | Ships (the slice)                              | Demo                  |
#   | f6.1 | the engine served as a web app                 | hit the root, see a page |
#   | f6.5 | the rendered catalog (index, course_card, ...) | browse the catalog    |

# A rung line decomposes into four facets — the fourth is always "none".
line = "f6.1 — the engine served as a web app"
rung      = "f6.1"            # names the rung
milestone = "1 · Ship the catalog"  # belongs to a milestone
spec      = "f6.1.md"          # points at a spec
behaviour = :none             # the line defines no behaviour

# All of it under the master invariant: the web layer calls only the Portal facade.

Recap — six parts, no behaviour

A chapter roadmap carries six parts: what it delivers, the start/end handoff, the architecture decision, the milestones, the per-iteration table, and the named open decisions. Each rung line within the arc names its rung, belongs to a milestone, points at a spec, and defines no behaviour — all over one master invariant.
