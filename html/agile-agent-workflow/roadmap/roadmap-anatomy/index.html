A3.3 · The roadmap layer

Anatomy of a roadmap.md

A chapter roadmap is a structured document, not a to-do list. Read a real one part by part and its shape becomes a template you can reuse.

The exemplar is the Portal's real web chapter — F6 (Phoenix) — and its phoenix.roadmap.md: nine rungs f6.1…f6.9, three milestones, each rung a line that points at a spec and defines no behaviour, all under one master invariant. Pick a part on the right to see what it carries.

The six parts The three dives References

Six parts, one invariant

A chapter roadmap answers six questions in order, and each is its own section in phoenix.roadmap.md. What we are delivering states the destination. Where this starts and ends names the handoff in and the end state out. Architecture decision records the one structural choice and its reversible cost. The delivery arc groups the rungs into three milestones. Per-rung iterations is the table where each rung ships a slice with a demo and a harness. Seams & open decisions names what is not yet decided and where it will be.

Over all six sits the master invariant, quoted verbatim from the file: “The web layer calls only the Portal facade and renders only the closed %Portal.Error{} set. No controller, LiveView, plug, or template names Portal.Engine, a repo, or GenServer.call.” It is the rule every rung holds, and — in the file's own words — “This single rule is what makes the ladder cheap”.

the principle

A roadmap has a fixed anatomy — deliverable, handoff, architecture decision, milestones, the per-iteration table, and the named open decisions — and reading those parts is how you learn to write one.
