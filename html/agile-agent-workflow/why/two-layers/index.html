A1.04 · Why an Agile Agent Workflow

Two layers

The framework has two planning layers, and keeping them apart is the whole discipline. A roadmap plans how we deliver; a spec defines what we build and how we know it is right. The roadmap plans the spec; the spec builds the system.

The roadmap layer is coarse and re-plannable — an ordered sequence of rungs that points at specs and never defines behaviour. The spec layer is fine and authoritative — the single source of truth, edited only by feedback, that every other artifact derives from. Three dives: the roadmap, the spec, and the one rule that keeps the two apart.

Two layers over a core

The framework stacks two layers over the system it builds. On top, the roadmap answers how we deliver: the milestones, the iterations, the order of rungs. It points at the specs below it and never defines behaviour, so it stays coarse and re-plannable. In the middle, the spec answers what we build and how we know it is right: for each rung, a precise definition and its acceptance. It is the single source of truth, edited only by feedback.

The relationship runs straight down: the roadmap plans the spec; the spec builds the domain core that actually ships. Two questions, two layers, two cadences — and the discipline is keeping them apart, so delivery can be re-planned without disturbing what a rung means, and a rung's meaning can be sharpened without re-planning delivery.

the principle

Separate the plan of delivery from the definition of correctness: a coarse, re-plannable roadmap over a fine, authoritative spec that is the single source of truth.
