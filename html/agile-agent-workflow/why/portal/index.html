Meet the project: Portal

One running project carries the whole course. Portal is a learning platform — courses, enrollments, lessons, progress, payments — assembled as a stack of surfaces over one domain core, and carried from an empty repository to production.

The surfaces are five and they share a centre: a branded store that mints and decodes every id, an event-sourced engine behind one facade, a Phoenix web app, a Telegram bot, and a student dashboard. Each surface calls only the facade, never the core directly, so the stack grows without the surfaces entangling. This module frames Portal three ways: what it is, what carrying it zero→production demands, and how the climb is taken one provable rung at a time.

Surfaces over a facade The three dives References

Surfaces over one facade

Portal is not a fresh domain per topic. It is one platform every example is practised on, so the work accumulates instead of resetting. At its centre sits a single domain core — courses, enrollments, lessons, progress, payments — reached through one facade. Around that facade sit five surfaces, each a different way to drive the same platform.

The discipline is the rule the figure makes visible: every surface calls the facade and only the facade. A surface never reaches into the core, and never into another surface. That keeps the blast radius of any change bounded to one box, and lets the platform gain a surface without disturbing the ones already there.
