The course itself

The triad is not only the shape of a Portal rung's spec. It is the shape of this chapter's own spec — a4.md, a4.stories.md, a4.llms.md.

Chapter A4 — the spec layer you are reading — is itself specified by three files, answering the same three questions one level up. The course is a worked example of the artifact it teaches. Select a question on the right to see both instances at once: the Portal rung file and the chapter file.

Two levels, one shape

The questions do not change with the subject. A specification of anything buildable and acceptable answers what & why & done, who wants what, and how to build — whether the subject is one rung of the Portal's engine or one chapter of a course. So chapter A4 is specified the same way a rung is: three files, the same three questions.

a4.md is the chapter contract: its Goal ("After A4, the reader can write a spec for a rung and the stories that accept it"), its Rationale (5W), Scope, Deliverables (the landing plus modules A4.1–A4.7), Invariants, and Definition of Done — the same six sections A4.3 teaches. a4.stories.md is the chapter's user's side: Connextra stories for three roles — the learner, the developer, and the Claude Author — with Given/When/Then, at chapter, module, and dive levels. a4.llms.md is the chapter's agent brief: the references, the module structure, the no-invent grounding, and the gate command an Author runs to verify each page.

a4.md
The chapter spec — what & why & done. Goal, Rationale (5W), Scope, Deliverables (the landing + A4.1…A4.7), Invariants, Definition of Done.
a4.stories.md
The chapter stories — who wants what. Connextra stories for the learner, the developer, and the Claude Author; Given/When/Then; at chapter, module, and dive levels.
a4.llms.md
The chapter agent brief — how to build it, with proof gates. References, the module structure, the no-invent grounding, the ten-gate command an Author self-verifies with.

A rung's triad and a chapter's triad are the same shape because the three questions are subject-independent. The next section checks the chapter holds itself to the rule.

Does the chapter close?

A4's Definition of Done says the chapter is done when its own triad reads as a spec that passes the traceability rule — the same closure A4.6 teaches. So the chapter is held to the artifact it teaches: each of its three files must answer its question. Step the three a4.* files and the readout reports whether the question is answered, and the running count toward a closed triad.

All three are present and each answers its question, so the chapter's triad is closed: three of three. The course does not exempt itself from the rule it argues for — it is specified by the triad it teaches.

The chapter does not exempt itself: a4.md, a4.stories.md, a4.llms.md answer all three questions, so its own triad closes — three of three.

The two triads, side by side

On the Portal, the rung F6.1 is specified by f6.1.*; in this course, the chapter A4 is specified by a4.*. Lined up, the two triads are the same three rows — the question is the constant, the file role is the constant, only the subject changes. A rung on one line, a chapter on the next.

The fragment below lines up both triads. It is markdown specification text, not code: the question as a comment, the two files as the matched pair. Reading down the comments reads the triad; reading across reads the two levels.

The principle

The triad specifies anything buildable and acceptable, at any level: the three questions are subject-independent, so a chapter is specified the same way a rung is.

Recap — the triad, applied to itself

Chapter A4 is specified by a4.md, a4.stories.md, and a4.llms.md — the same three files, answering the same three questions, that specify a Portal rung. The questions are subject-independent, so a rung and a chapter take the same shape, and the chapter's own triad closes: three of three. The triad is the unit of a specification, whatever the subject.
