INVEST and invariants

A story carries more than its sentence. Its INVEST line names the invariants it encodes and whether it is testable — the link from a story to the rule it must not break.

INVEST is six properties — Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small, Testable — and on the line a story states which invariants it encodes, plus a Priority and a Size. Two parts matter for the spec contract: which invariants a story exercises, and whether it is testable. What the INVEST line carries

Every story carries an INVEST line — the six properties (Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small, Testable) compressed to a short gloss, followed by a Priority, a Size, and the deliverables it implements. The two parts that bind it to the spec are these: which invariants the story encodes, and whether it is testable. An invariant is a property the increment must never break; encodes is the story's claim that its acceptance exercises that property.

A story that encodes no invariant and is not testable accepts nothing — it is a wish, not a check. Naming the invariant on the line is what ties the story back to the spec's Invariants section, so a reviewer can confirm that every invariant the rung declares is exercised by at least one testable story. That tie is what the next chapter, on invariants, makes its own subject; here it is read off the line.

INVEST
Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small, Testable — the six properties of a good story.
encodes
The invariants a story's acceptance exercises, named on its INVEST line (e.g. US2 encodes F6.1-INV1).
testable
The story states how it is checked; a non-testable story accepts nothing.
invariant
A property the increment must never break — declared in the spec, exercised by a story that encodes it.

A story names the invariants it encodes; the spec declares those invariants. The two meet on the INVEST line — the line is where a story claims its part of the rung's correctness.

Read an invariant back to the stories that exercise it

The hero figure read from the story side — pick a story, read the invariants it encodes. The figure below reads the other way: pick an invariant and see which stories encode it. An invariant exercised by at least one testable story is pinned by a check; an invariant no story encodes is merely asserted, the kind of gate the course warns against. F6.1's master invariant, INV1, is encoded by US2 and US3 — so the rule “the web layer calls only the facade” is exercised twice.

This is the reverse audit. The Coverage line proved every deliverable has a story; this proves every invariant has a story too. On the Portal — US2's INVEST line

On the Portal — the learning platform this course builds — the story F6.1-US2 in f6.1.stories.md carries the INVEST line below. It encodes F6.1-INV1, the master invariant: PortalWeb calls only the Portal facade and renders only the closed %Portal.Error{} set. Because US2 is testable by rendered output for three cases, that invariant is pinned by an acceptance check, not merely asserted. The markdown below carries US2's INVEST line verbatim.

# f6.1.stories.md — F6.1-US2's INVEST line (markdown, not code).
#  As a visitor, I want to open a course page for a given user…

# The INVEST line — testability and the invariant it encodes:
INVEST — valuable on its own (the first real page); testable by rendered output
         for the three cases; encodes F6.1-INV1.
Priority: must · Size: 3 · Implements deliverables: F6.1-D3, F6.1-D4, F6.1-D5.

# F6.1-INV1 (master): PortalWeb calls only the Portal facade and renders only the
# closed %Portal.Error{} set — exercised here, so the rule is checked, not asserted.

Recap — the line ties a story to the rules

A story's INVEST line names the invariants it encodes and whether it is testable. Read from the story side, a story shows the rules it exercises; read from the invariant side, an invariant shows the stories that pin it. An invariant no testable story encodes is asserted, not checked — the gap this reverse audit surfaces. With deliverable→story, the Coverage line, and the INVEST line all read, the module's move is complete: a spec's deliverables become a covered stories file whose every invariant is exercised.
