A2.02 · Decomposition

The Connextra form

A user story is one sentence that carries a whole way of working. The sentence is the form; the way of working is the three Cs.

The Connextra form — “As a role, I want a capability, so that a benefit” — states who gains, what they can do, and why it matters. The three Cs — Card, Conversation, Confirmation — make a story a promise of value with a conversation attached, not a contract frozen up front. One sentence, three slots

A user story is written in the Connextra form: “As a role, I want a capability, so that a benefit.” Three slots, three questions — for whom, what they can do, why it matters. The form is short on purpose: it states the promise of value and nothing more, so the story stays small and testable.

Around that sentence sit the three Cs. The Card is the written placeholder — the form plus a note or two. The Conversation is where the detail lives, deferred to the moment of building. The Confirmation is the acceptance check that says when the story is done. A story is a promise plus a conversation, not a contract written before the work.
