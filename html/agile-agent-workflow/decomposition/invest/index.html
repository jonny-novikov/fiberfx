A2.03 · Decomposition

I N V E S T

Six yes/no tests a user story passes before it is handed to the Author. One No is not a defect — it names the repair.

Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small, Testable. INVEST is a readiness gate read before a rung is built: a story that fails Small cannot fit one turn, and one that fails Testable cannot be accepted. Switch the story on the right to score it against the six. Three dives follow: the six tests, the four story smells, and the tension between Small and Independent.

The six tests The three dives References

Six tests, read one at a time

A story is the unit of work the Operator hands the Author. INVEST — Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small, Testable — is the checklist that says when a story is ready to hand over. Each letter is a separate yes/no question; a story is ready only when all six answer Yes.

The order of reading matters less than the discipline of reading each one. A story that fails S cannot fit one rung, so it cannot be specified or built in a single turn. A story that fails T has no concrete confirmation of done, so it cannot be accepted. An agent handed an unestimable, untestable instruction produces work no one can demonstrate. INVEST is read before any code is generated, because it is cheaper to repair a sentence than to unwind a rung.

the principle

A story is ready only when six independent tests all answer Yes. A single No is not a defect — it names the repair, before the rung is built.

on the Portal

“Enrol in a course” passes all six and ships in one rung. “Manage the whole catalogue” fails Small and Estimable, so it is split into the ladder before it is handed over.

INVEST is a readiness gate, not a grade: six yes/no tests, read before the rung, and the first No names the repair.

The three dives

Each dive takes one move, in the arc score → diagnose → resolve the tension: score a story against the six, diagnose the four recurring smells when a story fails, and resolve the one tension inside INVEST itself — Small pulling against Independent. Read them in order — A2.03.1 starts.
