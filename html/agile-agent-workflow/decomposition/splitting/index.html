A2.05 · Decomposition

Splitting what is too big

When a story fails Small or Estimable, the repair is not to shrink the words. It is to split the story into several that each still ship value.

INVEST (A2.03) reads a story and returns a verdict; a No on Small or Estimable means the story is too big for one rung. The fix is a vertical split — by workflow step, business rule, happy and sad path, or operation — that cuts the story into shippable ones without slicing it into horizontal layers no one can demo. Switch the candidate on the right to read whether it fits one rung or must be split. Three dives follow: when to split, the patterns, and vertical not horizontal.

What a split is The three dives References

A split is a transformation, not a trim

A split takes one story too big for a rung and returns several that fit, with no work lost: the parts sum to the whole. It is not the same as trimming a sentence shorter. Trimming changes the words; splitting changes the number of stories. The trigger is an INVEST failure — a No on Small or Estimable — read before any code is generated, because it is cheaper to cut a story than to unwind a rung.

A good split holds two properties at once. Every part passes Small and Estimable, so it fits one turn of the loop. And every part stays valuable and demoable end to end, so it is a story, not a fragment. The patterns in this module — by workflow step, by business rule, by happy and sad path, by operation — all cut vertically, down through the Portal’s store, domain, and surface, so each slice is a whole capability. The anti-pattern, cutting by horizontal layer, is small but leaves fragments no role can use.

the principle

A story too big for one rung is split, not trimmed: several parts that each pass Small and Estimable and each still deliver value, summing to the original whole.
