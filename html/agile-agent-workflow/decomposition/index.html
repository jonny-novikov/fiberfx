Decomposition: from vision to user stories

A vision is a destination, not a plan. This chapter turns it into a dependency-ordered ladder of small, valuable, testable user stories — each one a thin slice the loop can build and prove before the next begins. Decomposition is the human work the agent cannot do for you, and it is what makes every later rung tractable.

By the end of A2 you can take a one-line product vision and write it as a backlog: value not tasks, the Connextra story form and the three Cs, the INVEST test for a good story, Given/When/Then acceptance criteria, how to split a story that is too big, and how to order the result into a value ladder — all practised on the Portal.

The shape of decomposition

Decomposition is the move from a vision to a backlog: a list of stories small enough to build in one pass, valuable enough to demo, and ordered so each rung stands on the ones below it. A story is not a task. A task names work to do; a story names a change in what someone can do once the work lands. Stated that way, every story is independently demonstrable, and the order between them is a ladder of value rather than a checklist of chores.

The interactive below splits one Portal vision into that ladder. Step up the rungs and the readout reports what the slice delivers, what it depends on, and the acceptance check that proves it — the same three questions every story in this chapter must answer.
