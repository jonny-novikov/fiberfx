Order the backlog

The stories are ready but unordered. This dive applies the value ladder: order them so each rung depends only on rungs below it and the system is runnable after every one.

Order the Portal stories by dependency across the five surfaces — the store and the engine underlie every rung, the web and dashboard render the rungs, and the bot is a parallel surface that reuses them. The result is the canonical ladder — browse, enrol, open a lesson, track progress — carried into Part III. Step up the rungs on the right to read what is runnable at each.

Order by dependency, runnable at every rung

Dives 1 and 2 produced a set of ready stories, but a set is not a backlog — a backlog has an order. The value ladder supplies it: order the stories so each rung depends only on rungs below it, and the system is runnable after every one. Order across the five Portal surfaces. The branded store and the event-sourced engine behind one facade sit under everything; the web app and the dashboard render the rungs; the bot is a parallel surface over the same facade.

The order falls out of the dependencies. Browse the catalogue depends on nothing the learner sees, so it is the foundation. Enrol depends on browse — a learner can only enrol in a course they can see. Open a lesson depends on enrol — a lesson belongs to an enrolled course. Track progress (the slices from the previous dive) depends on opening lessons. This is the canonical Portal ladder, fixed for the rest of the course.

rung 1
Browse the catalogue (web, over the engine). The foundation — depends on nothing the learner sees.
rung 2
Enrol in a course (web → engine). Depends on rung 1: enrol only in a course you can browse.
rung 3
Open a lesson (web → engine). Depends on rung 2: a lesson belongs to an enrolled course.
rung 4
Track progress (the split slices, read on the dashboard). Depends on rung 3: progress is recorded against opened lessons.

The store and engine are not rungs on this ladder — they are the base every rung stands on, built in the companion course. The bot reuses rungs 1–3 over the same facade without adding a dependency.

Why the order has to be right

A dependency order is not a preference; it is what keeps the ladder demoable. Put a rung below the one it depends on and the lower rung has nothing runnable to stand on. Order enrol below browse, and the demo at that step has no visible course to enrol in — the rung cannot be shown working, so the increment is not runnable.

Switch the order below. The dependency-correct order satisfies every rung’s dependency below it and is runnable at every step; the mis-order places a rung beneath its dependency. The readout reports how many rungs break and whether the ladder is runnable end to end.

The backlog, written out

On the Portal, the ordered backlog reads as below: four rungs over the store and engine, each tagged by the surface it touches, each runnable once the rungs beneath it ship. This is the artifact the whole chapter was for — the dependency-ordered, tested ladder the course specifies, briefs, and builds from Part III onward. The store and engine that hold it together are built in the companion course; the backlog is the human work that decides what gets built and in what order.

# The Portal backlog — ordered by dependency, runnable at every rung.
#   base: the branded store + event-sourced engine behind one facade.

1 · browse the catalogue    # web · depends on nothing the learner sees
2 · enrol in a course       # web · depends on 1 (browse)
3 · open a lesson           # web · depends on 2 (enrol)
4 · track progress          # dashboard · depends on 3 · the split slices from A2.07.2

# The bot is a parallel surface over the same facade: it reuses rungs 1-3,
# adding no new dependency. Carried into Part III as the delivery plan.
the principle

A backlog is a dependency-ordered ladder where each rung adds usable value, depends only on rungs below it, and leaves the system runnable.

→
on the Portal

The stories order into browse → enrol → open a lesson → track progress over the store and engine, the bot a parallel surface — runnable at every rung, the backlog Part III builds.

Ordering turns a set of ready stories into a ladder the loop can climb one provable rung at a time.

Recap — the chapter, in one backlog

The workshop is done. The one-line Portal vision ran through value, the Connextra form, INVEST, and Given/When/Then to a first story set; the too-big story split into vertical slices, each re-tested to six of six; and the value ladder ordered the result by dependency across the five surfaces into a backlog runnable at every rung. That backlog — browse, enrol, open a lesson, track progress, over the store and engine, with the bot a parallel surface — is what the course specifies, briefs, and builds from here. Chapter A2 is complete; the work it produced is the input to Part III.
