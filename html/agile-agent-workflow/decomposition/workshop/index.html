Workshop: decomposing Portal

The Portal’s web surface, decomposed for real

The earlier modules taught the moves on toy stories. The capstone applies them where they were actually used: the Portal’s web surface — chapter F6 in the companion course. Its one-line vision, serve the Portal to people, was decomposed into nine vertical rungs, f6.1 through f6.9. Every rung ships one capability a real role can use, calls only the unchanged Portal facade, and carries its own four artifacts: a line in phoenix.roadmap.md, a spec f6.N.md, Given/When/Then acceptance in f6.N.stories.md, and an agent brief f6.N.llms.md.

Drag the slider to walk the roadmap. The readout answers the four questions a decomposition has to settle for every rung — what it delivers, when it was specified and shipped (from the repository’s git history), why (which milestone it serves), and how it is proven — read straight off the real f6.N files. Each rung is taught and built as its own chapter in the companion course, under /elixir/phoenix — the bootstrap at /elixir/phoenix/lifecycle, persistence at /elixir/phoenix/ecto, and so on through the dashboard.

The three dives

Each dive carries part of the decomposition by hand on the real F6 chapter, in the arc what & why → how → when: read the vision into its rung ladder of real stories, show one rung as its four artifacts and its proof, then order the rungs into milestones and watch the specs evolve in git. Read them in order — A2.07.1 starts.

A2.07 closes the chapter on the real thing. The F6 decomposition walked above — nine rungs, four artifacts each, ordered into three milestones — is the kind of backlog Part III, the roadmap layer, plans into delivery. Each rung is then specified, built, and proven in the companion /elixir Phoenix chapter — F6.4 contexts at /elixir/phoenix/contexts, F6.6 LiveView at /elixir/phoenix/liveview; this workshop shows their decomposition.
