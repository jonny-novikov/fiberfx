One log, three replays.

Put three cursors on the same commit log: a booting process at the last snapshot, a replica at its own LSN, a feed subscriber at its cursor. Advancing any of them runs the identical step — apply(commit) — one LSN at a time toward the head. Different starts, one operation. Advance them and watch.

Why one mechanism is enough

graft.design.md

Collapsing three features into one fold is not a tidy diagram — it is the reason the engine is small. There is a single, well-tested replay path; boot, catch-up, and the feed are call sites that hand it a starting LSN. Determinism does the rest: the same commits applied in the same order reach the same state, so a replica that replays the primary’s log is the primary, and a feed subscriber sees exactly what a recovering process would. The next two dives are just this fold with the starting LSN and byte source pinned down — green boot from the local snapshot, replica catch-up from remote segments.
