### T-2 — the seed re-read from the shop lens (the cost-per-key spread is real and economically load-bearing)

Derivation (Venus-Revenue-Shop), grounded in economy.packages.md + economy.ex:11:
- 1000-pack: 9999⭐ / 1000 keys = 9.999⭐/key × $0.013 = ~$0.13 GROSS cost/key (50% discount).
- 5-pack: 99⭐ / 5 keys = 19.8⭐/key × $0.013 = ~$0.26 GROSS cost/key (0% discount).
- In-game key VALUE is fixed at 12¢ (10💎 × @cents_per_diamond 1.2, economy.ex:11; to_cents economy.ex:22).
So the deep-discount key is bought ~AT its in-game value (~13¢ vs 12¢); the shallow key at ~2× (~26¢). The WAC is the only honest "cost per key" for a fungible balance blended across packages. Shop-lens consequence: the discount ladder is SAFE to pair with cm.8 withdrawals ONLY because a per-key cost basis (G1 WAC) floors the payout rate — without it a 50%-off key + a face-rate withdrawal is a 2× money pump. The store fee (~32% mobile / ~3% desktop, economy.packages.md) makes "cost basis" ambiguous (gross-paid vs net-received) — a shop-lens design point resolved in F5/F3.

## {cm-revenue-model-learnings} Learnings

### L-1 — bitmapist counting edge is FORWARD, not as-built (NO-INVENT guard for F4)

Probed on disk (Venus-Revenue-Shop): `infra/codemojex-bitmapist/` is ABSENT from the working tree. The bitmapist substrate is described only in the auth-flow design-ahead KB (`kb/auth-flow/README.md` G8 + `auth.design.A-consumer-lens.md:57,81,425,628`), which is itself "design-ahead, uncommitted, for review" and names the marker/dashboard wiring as "roadmap P1-P3 (unbuilt)". Consequence for the shop-lens F4 (Analytics Engine): any proposal routing analytics through the bitmapist counting edge (`:6400`, RESP2, Go redigo client, cohorts active/registered/played/paid) MUST be framed forward-tense as a PROPOSED edge grounded in the auth-flow KB — never as shipped infra. The `AEV` analytics brand is likewise forward (free namespace). The as-built analytics seed is real and on disk: `Wallet.revenue_breakdown/1` (wallet.ex:351) + `house_balance/0..1` (wallet.ex:325).

### L-2 — Store the TOTAL basis (not the average) → re-blend is pure addition + conservation is STRUCTURAL

The elegant ledger result: if `players` carries `key_cost_basis_micros` = the total canonical cost of CURRENT keys (not the WAC), then on buy `basis += canonical_cost` is pure ADDITION — no weighted-average division, no float WAC, no re-blend rounding on acquire. The average emerges only at read (basis/keys). On spend of S of K: `cost_out=floor(basis·S/K); basis-=cost_out` — the remainder STAYS in basis (not lost), washing out at spend-all (S=K ⇒ cost_out=basis exactly ⇒ basis→0, zero strand). So dust is bounded <1µUSD per balance and self-cleaning — conservation is STRUCTURAL, the cm.6 one-floor-complement discipline applied to cost. This is the F2-Arm-A + F5-dust-rule unification; it eliminates the float-WAC hazard entirely.

### L-3 — The brief's conservation candidate OMITS two terms a game with a pool + winnings cannot omit

The brief's candidate `Σ gross_pay_in == Σ realized_revenue + deferred_liability + Σ withdrawn_at_cost + dust` is incomplete: (1) it omits B_win — keys minted from WON 💎 (convert_to_keys, wallet.ex:157) are a basis INJECTION not from pay-in (valued 10💎=1key, 💎=1.2¢); without it the identity fails for any player who has won. (2) It lumps ALL spend into "realized revenue," but spent keys SPLIT by destination: golden guess fees + buy-in pool portion (entry_fee_split_keys, economy.ex:63) fund the PRIZE POOL — a LIABILITY transfer, NOT revenue; only paid-room guess fees + the buy-in house cut (wallet.ex:271) are realized. Lumping overstates revenue by the pool transfer. The corrected identity carries B_win + splits realized_revenue_basis from pool_transfer_basis. This is the load-bearing ledger-lens refinement — and exactly the floor-divergence class the cm.6 property test (vs fixed examples) exists to catch.

### L-4 — Two NO-INVENT catches + the WAC-normalization-does-NOT-break-cm.7-D-6 distinction

(a) The brief's bitmapist path `infra/codemojex-bitmapist` is the README NAME; the on-disk directory is `infra/cm-bitmapist/` (verified — a Go bitmapist4 port). Corrected in the doc.
(b) The crucial cross-fork distinction the synthesis must hold: normalizing the WAC to canonical µUSD (F3-A) does NOT violate cm.7 D-6 F5 "store native, convert at read" — because they govern DIFFERENT ledgers. The revenue_ledger keeps the native-unit gross ("what arrived," never normalized, frozen — the audit); the WAC is a SEPARATE derived cost basis in canonical units, computed once from the PINNED order rate (cm.7 D-4, reproducible). The lossy step D-6 forbids (baking a rate into THE REVENUE LEDGER) is never taken — only the new derived basis is canonical. Two orthogonal ledgers in different units; the whole model is the discipline keeping them reconcilable (F5) without the second corrupting the first.

## {cm-revenue-model-alternatives} Alternatives

### V-1 — F1 Arm B (full accrual) STEELMAN — the strongest case against the shop lens's Hybrid (C)

The accounting-correct view: a player who buys 1000 keys and plays none has given the platform CASH but not REVENUE — a deferred liability, not earned income. Booking gross-at-pay-in as recognized revenue overstates earned revenue at any instant (textbook deferred-revenue). A serious finance function + an auditor expect accrual: recognize keys_spent · WAC at the spend events, carry outstanding keys as a liability. WHY the shop lens ranks it BELOW Hybrid: it semantically reshapes the byte-frozen RVL purchase row (which means "revenue recognized") into a "deposit" — G2 forbids — and muddies the cash/liquidity view the 21-day-hold withdrawal needs; the accrual NUMBER is obtainable as a READ-time projection (Hybrid) without any of that reshape. The live fork for the Operator: is recognition-timing a STORAGE decision (B — reshape the ledger) or a REPORTING decision (C — project accrual over the cash store)? The shop lens holds it is reporting.

### V-2 — F2 Arm B (cost-lots ledger) STEELMAN — the ledger lens's likely pick

Auditor-grade provenance: each acquisition is a lot row (COST brand, FIFO/specific-id), so a spend consumes lots by a rule and every key traces to its purchase + price. The maximal-fidelity cost model; the substrate for tax-lot optimization. WHY the shop lens ranks it BELOW the running field (A): the fungible-keys wallet has NO natural consumption order (players.keys is one count, not lots — cm.7 A-7), so FIFO is an IMPOSED fiction buying provenance the blended-margin reads never use (margin never matches a spent key to a purchase). And the provenance ALREADY exists in orders/order_transactions (every ORD/OTX is append-only with price_minor + pinned rate) — so the audit trail is there regardless; the running field is a fast O(1) read OVER it, drift-proofed by a reconciliation property test (running field ≡ fold-over-orders, the cm.6 precedent). A COST table duplicates orders and taxes every spend with a lot walk. The live fork: does WAC need lot-level provenance (B) or is the order history + a reconciled running aggregate (A) sufficient? The shop lens holds A suffices for every revenue-model read.

### V-3 — F3 Arm B (per-rail cost basis) STEELMAN — the audit-purist case + the F3/D-6-F5 tension it raises

Exact to the rail, no rate baked into the stored basis: a WAC per currency keeps each rail's cost in its own native unit, honoring cm.7 D-6 F5 (store native, convert at read) literally — the ledger lens's strongest F3 frame. WHY the shop lens rejects it: a single FUNGIBLE key balance cannot carry 4 cost bases — a spend would have to PICK a rail (a consumption-order rule on fungible keys), and the product STILL needs ONE comparable number for blended margin + the withdrawal floor, so the canonicalization is merely deferred to read-time while a spend-time selection rule is ADDED — strictly worse than canonicalizing once, at acquisition, at the PINNED rate (Arm A/C). The tension Arm B exposes (and Arm A/C resolves): D-6 F5 governs the REVENUE gross (native, audit = what arrived, frozen); the WAC is a SEPARATE figure whose purpose is comparability, so it MUST be canonical — two figures, two units, both audit-safe (the WAC bakes the PINNED order rate, re-derivable). The live fork the Operator weighs: is "store native, convert at read" a law over ALL money figures (B), or only over the revenue ledger, with the cost basis canonical-by-necessity (A/C)?

### V-4 — F4 Arm C (the cohort/counting edge) STEELMAN + the LAYERED-Engine insight (the shop lens's core F4 contribution)

ARPU/LTV/retention + the count-funnel (registered→active→played→paid; pay-in→spend→pool→withdrawal conversion) are NOT SQL-aggregate shaped — they are cohort-bitmap shaped (roaring bitmaps, AndCount/OrCount, RetentionRow), and rebuildable-by-replay (the AEV property: never authoritative, reconstructable). Forcing them onto the money Postgres projection (Arm A) bloats it with a workload PG serves poorly. The shop-lens insight (the genuine F4 contribution): the Analytics Engine is NOT one-of-three substrates — it is LAYERED, each operator read sitting where its CONSISTENCY need lands it: money funnel (gross/realized/deferred/margin/liability) = penny-exact Postgres (A, the launch FLOOR, extends revenue_breakdown/1); cohort/LTV funnel = countable + rebuildable bitmapist edge (C, FORWARD — the AEV brand + bitmapist :6400, PROBED ABSENT on disk, PROPOSED only); hot-dashboard serving = EchoStore cache (B, later, only if hot). This is the mesh.8.1 CAP-segmentation discipline applied to the analytics surface. The ledger lens will (rightly) say the money reads are just SQL over the frozen ledger — TRUE for the floor (= Arm A, ranked first); the disagreement is SCOPE: the shop lens additionally NAMES the forward cohort + serving tiers (AEV, LiveAdmin) so the forward rungs have a target. Naming a forward tier is not building it.

### V-5 — F5 Statement 2 (keep cm.6's keys-unit conservation as THE identity) STEELMAN — preserve, do not replace

The proven, at-rest, store-native invariant: Σ player_key_debits == Σ house_key_credits + Σ pool_key_portions (cm.6 D-3), in keys, over three observable columns; it bakes NO rate, it is shipped + property-tested (cm.6 Apollo found + closed the M5 floor-divergence survivor here). The ledger lens's likely F5 frame: state conservation in ONE store-native unit, resist a canonical-USD identity as rate-baking. WHY the shop lens ranks it as PRESERVE-not-replace (alongside Statement 1, not instead): it is the GOLDEN-ROOM keys-flow (pool) conservation, NOT the pay-in cost-basis conservation — it says nothing about deferred liability, realized revenue, or the withdrawal floor, the figures the broadened scope (G5) + cm.8 require. The reconcile: cm.6's keys identity stays the AT-REST invariant (untouched); the canonical-USD WAC conservation (Statement 1: Σ gross_pay_in == deferred + realized + withdrawn_at_cost + dust) is the READ-TIME revenue-model invariant, a property test at the PINNED rates (audit-safe like the cm.7 §8.1 read-time USD roll-up), dust pinned to one bucket (cm.5 add_dust). Both hold; neither bakes a stored total. The live fork: is the revenue-model conservation owed in keys-native-only (2) or additionally in canonical-USD-read-time (1)? The shop lens holds BOTH — 1 is what makes the funnel reconcile to the penny + floors cm.8 solvency.

### V-6 — F1 Arm A (cash-basis) STEELMAN — the shop lens's likely pick, chosen-against by Arm C

Strongest case FOR recognizing gross at pay-in, full stop: for a consumable game token many operators DO recognize at point of sale (a key is a sale, not a refundable deposit); zero new accounting machinery; it is the tax/operational truth (cash received) and what already ships (cm.7 D-3, house_balance/0..1 wallet.ex:340). Ledger-lens chosen-against: it leaves the deferred-revenue liability (outstanding keys · WAC) INVISIBLE — incomplete for a prepaid instrument. Arm C dominates it by KEEPING this exact cash number (frozen) and ADDING the accrual split as a derived projection over the ruled WAC (G1) — no second store, no frozen-row touch. Retained as the cash LAYER inside C, not discarded.

### V-7 — F2 Arm B (cost-lots `LOT` ledger) + F3 Arm B (per-rail basis) STEELMAN — chosen-against by Arm A

F2-B steelman: a `LOT` row per acquisition gives exact lot-level provenance → FIFO/LIFO/specific-id + per-lot margin audit, the richest cost trail. F3-B steelman: a per-rail WAC is exact to the rail — no rate ever enters the basis. Ledger-lens chosen-against (both): G1 ruled WEIGHTED-AVERAGE, under which consumption order is IRRELEVANT (every key carries the blend), so the lot machinery (provenance, exhaustion order, the mutable `remaining_keys`) buys nothing the policy uses while BREAKING the append-only ledger discipline; and a fungible key pool cannot carry N bases without re-importing the consumption-order problem WAC exists to delete. The per-rail/per-package provenance the shop lens wants already lives IMMUTABLY in `orders` (package_id+rail+price_minor+pinned rate) + the F4 analytics projection — not on the hot fungible balance. Arm A (one running field, store total basis, re-blend by addition) wins: O(1), on-the-account (G1 literal), integer-exact, append-aligned, and the exact O(1) single-canonical-unit read cm.8's withdrawal seam needs.

### V-8 — F4 Arms B (EchoStore) + C (bitmapist) STEELMAN — NOT chosen-against; LAYERED beneath Arm A

The ledger lens does NOT reject the fast analytics layers — it layers them. B steelman: EchoStore near-cache gives sub-ms glanceable counters (live pool, revenue ticker). C steelman: `infra/cm-bitmapist` (verified on disk — branded-id-native bitmapist4 port, Offset=Hash32(Decode(id)), Mark/AndCount/RetentionRow) is the RIGHT tool for the actor funnel (buyers→spenders→winners→withdrawers, retention) and ALREADY EXISTS. The ledger-lens position: A (a Postgres view/materialized projection over revenue_ledger+transactions+orders+the WAC basis) is the SoR-EXACT FINANCIAL layer (the books, by construction, revenue_breakdown/1 seed) and is non-negotiable for revenue/deferred/liability/margin; B is an eventually-consistent CACHE that cites A (coherence-risky for reconciliation, fine for counters); C is the actor-COUNT funnel (one-way offset, counts actors not money) fed by AEV events. The synthesis carries all three, each citing the one below — the layering IS the convergence with the shop lens.
