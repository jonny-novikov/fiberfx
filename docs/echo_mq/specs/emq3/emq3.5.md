## 0 · The slice — what emq3.5 builds, and why the archive is a store-side fold-then-trim

The tier ([`../../emq.streams.md`](../../emq.streams.md)) ships **event streams on the certified wire under the v2 laws, no second protocol**. Its three milestones are **S1 the writer** (emq3.1–3.2) → **S2 the readers** (emq3.3–3.4) → **S3 the memory** (emq3.5–3.6). emq3.5 is the FIRST rung of S3: the tier's stated **"deep history without resident memory"** need ([`../../emq.streams.md`](../../emq.streams.md) §The needs: *"Deep history without resident memory — stream segments folded to local CubDB and streamed to Tigris by the `EchoStore.Graft` engine (emq3.5)"*) and §The durable-archive answer (*"The archive tier (emq3.5) folds trimmed stream segments into the `EchoStore.Graft` engine's local CubDB and lets the engine stream those pages natively to Tigris S3 — one durable path, one knob (`remote_cfg`), not a sidecar … Box-loss recovery is the engine's lazy fetch of `segments/{SEG}` frames from Tigris — and the fold being a property of the engine already in place is what makes the archive nearly free"*).

**The never-deleted problem, and why retention alone is not the answer.** emq3.4 bounds a stream's memory by trimming entries past a declared window — a SAFETY (bounded memory) the operator declares per stream. But a trim is a PERMANENT removal: once `XTRIM` runs, the trimmed entries are gone (F-2). For a stream the operator wants bounded in RAM but DEEP in history (a recorded game's event log; an audit trail), retention alone loses the history. emq3.5 answers this: before a segment is trimmed, it is FOLDED durably into the native engine, where it survives box loss (the engine streams it to Tigris) and reads back beside the live tail. The trim stays bounded; the history stays whole. **Fold-before-trim is the load-bearing invariant** — it is the only ordering that loses no record (F-2): the consumer reads the slice, commits it, advances `W`, THEN trims.

**The archive lands store-side — the dependency law forces it (D-1, EBV3-3).** The engine (`EchoStore.Graft.*`) and the page store (`cubdb`) live in `echo/apps/echo_store`; `echo_mq` has NO `echo_store` dependency and NO `cubdb` (`echo_store/mix.exs:24-35` declares `echo_data` + `echo_mq` + `echo_wire` + `exqlite` + `cubdb`; the arrow is `echo_store → echo_mq`, one-way). The bus CANNOT call the store. So the fold consumer, the page-index landing, the merge-read, and the `W` reader all live store-side; at most an OPTIONAL `emq:{q}:stream:<name>:archived` polyglot cache key lands bus-side (a cache, never the source of truth — EBV3-5). **This is the FIRST stream rung to reach store-side** — the precision on the trim→fold→merge boundary is the whole rung. The Operator ruled the two-app boundary D-1 (Arm A, the seam fork surfaced BEFORE any spawn); the third-app rule still bars `echo_graft` / `codemojex` / `echo_bot` / `mix.lock`.

**The engine the fold targets is the native `EchoStore.Graft`, COEXIST-canonical and UNTOUCHED (D-1, EBV3-2/9).** The fold commits through the public `EchoStore.Graft.VolumeServer.commit/3` (`volume_server.ex:50`) — the in-process native engine, the production-default durability (EBV3-9 ruled Graft-canonical). ## 1 · The fold contract — read-slice → `commit/3` into `@archive_base` → advance `W` → THEN `trim/4` (fold-then-trim, INV1, the load-bearing ordering)

emq3.5 builds a store-side fold consumer that, for a declared archived stream, performs the **fold-then-trim** cycle. The contract, stated as precondition / postcondition / invariant:

| Step | What happens | The surface (real or forward-tense) |
|---|---|---|
| **1 · read the about-to-expire slice** | `XRANGE` the stream's tail span that the retention policy is about to trim — recovering each record's branded `EVT` id from the stored `id` field, in mint order. | `EchoMQ.Stream.read/3..6` (`stream.ex:270`, SHIPPED) — `{:ok, [{branded, fields_map}]}` in mint order. The slice is `(W, floor]` — records minted after the current archive frontier `W` and at-or-below the trim floor. |
| **2 · fold into the engine** | `commit/3` a staged page map into the Volume at the `@archive_base` range — each page carrying one record's branded `EVT` id + its claims-only fields. The commit is durable + (with `remote_cfg`) streamed to Tigris. | `EchoStore.Graft.VolumeServer.commit/3` (`volume_server.ex:50`, SHIPPED) → `{:ok, lsn}`; `@archive_base` (forward-tense, the `@obx_base` precedent `plugins/graft.ex:46`). |
| **3 · advance `W`** | Persist the branded `EVT` id of the highest-folded record as the new archive frontier `W` (a CubDB frontier key and/or carried in the page payload). `W` is monotone (the order theorem — records fold in mint order). | the archive-frontier writer (forward-tense — F-1; `W` is a branded id, NOT `head_lsn`). |
| **4 · THEN trim** | ONLY after the commit succeeds AND `W` is advanced, `trim/4` the now-archived span (a `MINID` floor at-or-below `W`, or a `MAXLEN` keeping the live tail). The trimmed entries are ALREADY in the engine — no record is lost (INV1). | `EchoMQ.Stream.trim/4` (`stream.ex:213`, SHIPPED) → `{:ok, removed_count}`. |

**INV1 — fold-before-trim (the load-bearing no-loss invariant).** A record is trimmed from the live stream ONLY AFTER it has been committed to the engine and `W` has advanced past it. *Check (POSITIVE, never a no-op):* the store-side fold suite appends K records to a stream, runs ONE fold-then-trim cycle, and asserts (a) every trimmed record is READABLE from the archive (the engine's `@archive_base` range, in mint order — segment fold == stream slice, INV2); (b) the live stream retains ONLY the un-folded tail; (c) the union of archive ∪ live-tail == the original K records, no gap / no overlap (INV3). A cycle that trims a record NOT yet in the engine is a LOUD failure — the Director's mutation battery REORDERS the steps (trim-before-fold) and asserts the suite catches the loss.

**Step ordering is the whole game (F-2).** Because `XTRIM` returns only a count (it cannot hand back what it deleted), the fold MUST precede the trim — there is no recovery after the trim. The consumer OWNS both calls (it reads the slice, folds, advances `W`, trims) precisely so the ordering is enforced in ONE process, not split across a bus event the store cannot observe (EBV3-3 — "no injected bus-side callback; the no-loss invariant must not live where the bus cannot verify it"). The trigger that STARTS a cycle — a tick, a depth watch, a hand-off from the emq3.4 driver — is **Arm 2** (the Director rules).

## 2 · The archive landing — a reserved high page range `@archive_base`, DISJOINT from `@obx_base`, the page payload carrying the branded id (INV4, F-LS-A / EBV3-1)

The fold commits each record as a CubDB page at a per-stream `@archive_base` range — the SAME mechanism the outbox uses at `@obx_base`, a SECOND disjoint reserved range:

1. `@obx_base = :erlang.bsl(1, 48)` is the outbox's reserved range (`plugins/graft.ex:46`, SHIPPED) — "far above any realistic business page count." The archive's `@archive_base` is a DISJOINT high range (forward-tense — a candidate is `:erlang.bsl(1, 49)` or a `bsl(3, 47)`-class constant; **the disjointness is the invariant, the exact constant is the build's to settle + pin**). Disjoint means: NO archive page index can collide with a business page or an outbox-intent page — the page axis is MULTIPLEXED (business pages + the outbox's `@obx_base` range + the archive's `@archive_base` range), GC indifferent to which range (the converged `local-store/cubdb` framing).
2. The page index is branded-id-MONOTONE: records fold in mint order (the order theorem extended to the archive — `EchoMQ.Stream.read/3..6` returns the slice in mint order, and the writer mints monotone `EVT` ids), so `@archive_base + n` for the n-th folded record is contiguous and ordered. A reverse-BYLEX-class scan of the `@archive_base` range reads the archive back in mint order — NO second index (the order theorem, Appendix F extended to the log).
3. The page PAYLOAD carries the record's branded `EVT` id + its claims-only fields (`:erlang.term_to_binary({branded, fields})` — the `EchoStore.Durability.Graft` `record/4` payload precedent, `plugins/graft.ex:84`). The branded id IS the canonical receipt (a polyglot reader recovers it without re-encoding — the claims-only contract).

**INV4 — `@archive_base` disjoint from `@obx_base` (no page collision).** No archive page index equals any outbox-intent page index or any business page index. *Check:* a unit assertion that `@archive_base > @obx_base + (max realistic intent count)` AND that an archive page and an outbox page at the same logical offset have DISTINCT CubDB indices; a positive integration assertion that a Volume carrying BOTH outbox intents AND archive pages reads each range back correctly (`idx >= @archive_base` filters the archive, `@obx_base <= idx < @archive_base` filters the outbox — the existing `plugins/graft.ex:161` filter pattern extended). The Director's mutation battery sets `@archive_base = @obx_base` and asserts the collision is CAUGHT (an archive page overwrites an intent, or vice versa).

## 3 · The merge-read — over an engine-derived watermark `W` (a branded `EVT` id), no gap / no overlap (INV3, F-1 / F-PLAT-B / EBV3-5)

A deep read of an archived stream spans the trim boundary: records below the boundary live in the engine (the archive), records above it live on the wire (the live tail). The merge-read returns their UNION:

1. **`W` is the branded `EVT` id of the highest-folded record (F-1).** It is read from the engine's archive frontier (a CubDB frontier key the fold advances, and/or the max branded id in the `@archive_base` range) — NEVER the integer `head_lsn` (that is the engine's page cursor, the WRONG type). emq3.5 ADDS the `W` reader (forward-tense): a store-side function `archive_frontier(vol_or_stream)` → `{:ok, branded_W} | :empty`.
2. **The split is exact (the order theorem).** Records with branded id ≤ `W` are folded (read from the engine's `@archive_base` range); records with branded id > `W` are live (read from the stream via `XRANGE`, the live tail — `W`'s `xadd_id` maps to the `XRANGE` lower bound via `Stream.Id.xadd_id/1`, `<ms>` the real Unix-ms). Because the fold is mint-ordered and fold-before-trim holds, `W` is precisely the seam: every record ≤ `W` is in the engine, every record > `W` is on the wire, and the two sets are DISJOINT and CONTIGUOUS (no gap, no overlap).
3. **No-gap/no-overlap is a CONSEQUENCE, not a check at read time (F-PLAT-B).** It follows from fold-before-trim (INV1 — nothing is trimmed before it is folded, so the live tail never has a gap below `W`) + the order theorem (the fold is mint-ordered, so `W` is a clean seam). The merge-read does NOT re-verify the boundary on every read; it trusts the invariant and splits on `W`.

**INV3 — the merge-read property: a read spanning the trim boundary returns archived ∪ live-tail, no gap / no overlap.** *Check:* the fold suite appends K records, folds-then-trims a prefix (so `W` is mid-stream), and a merge-read over the full range returns EXACTLY the K records in mint order — every record present once, none missing (no gap), none doubled (no overlap). A read of a range fully below `W` hits only the engine; a range fully above `W` hits only the wire; a range straddling `W` hits both and stitches at the seam.

**The optional bus-side `:archived` cache key (EBV3-5, NOT source-of-truth — Arm 4).** A bus-side `emq:{q}:stream:<name>:archived` key MAY cache `W` (so a polyglot reader can discover the seam without a store call) — but it is a CACHE, never the source of truth (the engine's frontier is). Whether emq3.5 builds it THIS rung (and adds its conformance scenario) or defers it is **Arm 4**. If it lands: it is a stock `SET`/`GET` over `Connector.command/3` (no new script, no `keyspace.ex` grammar edit — the key rides the existing `emq:{q}:stream:<name>:<sub>` form), its cleanup disposition is NAMED (it is overwritten on each fold and DELETEd when the stream is obliterated), and it adds +1 conformance (78), re-pinned in both pins. If deferred: the bus-side conformance is byte-frozen at 77 and `W` is store-side only.

## 4 · The fold consumer + box-loss restore — the store-side process, the engine's lazy Tigris fetch (INV5, F-ENG-B / EBV3-2/9)

**The fold consumer is store-side (D-1, EBV3-3 — Arm 1 carves "store-side process vs host-side wiring").** A `Committer`-shaped consumer (`committer.ex` the precedent) owns the fold-then-trim cycle: it knows the archived stream + its Volume, reads the slice, commits, advances `W`, trims. Whether it is a SUPERVISED `EchoStore.*` GenServer (owned by echo_store's supervision tree) or a HOST-WIRED consumer the application composes is **Arm 1** (the Director rules — F-ENG-B left it "store-side OR host-side"). Either way: NO injected bus-side callback (EBV3-3 — the no-loss invariant lives where the store can verify it, never where only the bus can see it).

**Box-loss restore is the engine's lazy Tigris fetch (INV5, the engine's existing property — EBV3-9).** The archive's durability is the engine's, not a new mechanism: with `remote_cfg` set, the `VolumeServer` starts a `Streamer` that ships `@archive_base` pages to Tigris as `segments/{SEG}` (`volume_server.ex:90`, `EchoStore.Graft.Streamer`). On box loss, a fresh node re-opens the Volume and the engine lazily re-fetches the segments — the archive reads back identically (INV5). The fold being a property of the engine ALREADY IN PLACE is what makes the archive nearly free ([`../../emq.streams.md`](../../emq.streams.md) §The durable-archive answer).

**INV5 — box-loss restore (drop local CubDB → restore → archive reads identical).** *Check (the determinism posture, Arm 5):* the LOCAL-engine path is unit-testable without live S3 — fold K records into a Volume, DROP the local CubDB dir, re-open the Volume from the same dir (or, for the offline proof, from a captured snapshot), and assert the archive reads back identically; the LIVE-Tigris path (a `:tigris`/`:valkey`-style tag) drops the dir and re-fetches `segments/{SEG}` from a live bucket. The honest determinism posture (Arm 5) states which is in the suite and which is tagged. The native engine is tested OFFLINE today (`durability_test.exs`, `async: true` — pure rollup/frontier/fencing) — the box-loss LOCAL path follows that precedent; the LIVE-Tigris path is tagged and run only when the bucket is configured.

## Goal

emq3.5 builds, across `echo/apps/echo_mq` + `echo/apps/echo_store` (the Operator-ruled two-app boundary, D-1), **the archive** for the Stream Tier:

1. **The store-side fold consumer (the fold-then-trim cycle, INV1, D-1).** A `Committer`-shaped consumer (supervised `EchoStore.*` GenServer OR host-wired — **Arm 1**) that, for a declared archived stream, reads the about-to-expire slice (`EchoMQ.Stream.read/3..6`), folds it into the engine (`VolumeServer.commit/3` at `@archive_base`), advances `W`, and THEN trims (`EchoMQ.Stream.trim/4`) — fold-BEFORE-trim, the no-loss invariant the bus cannot verify (no injected bus callback, EBV3-3). The trigger that starts a cycle is **Arm 2**.
2. **The archive landing — a reserved high page range `@archive_base`, DISJOINT from `@obx_base` (INV4, F-LS-A).** Each record folds as a CubDB page at `@archive_base + n` (the `@obx_base` precedent, `plugins/graft.ex:46`), the page payload carrying the branded `EVT` id + claims-only fields, branded-id-monotone (contiguous by the order theorem). The exact `@archive_base` constant is the build's to settle + pin; the disjointness is the invariant.
3. **The merge-read over an engine-derived `W` (INV3, F-1 / F-PLAT-B).** A store-side read returning archived ∪ live-tail, split on `W` = the branded `EVT` id of the highest-folded record (read from the engine's frontier — NOT the integer `head_lsn`); no-gap/no-overlap a CONSEQUENCE of fold-before-trim + the order theorem, never a per-read boundary check. The optional bus-side `:archived` cache key (a polyglot cache of `W`, never source-of-truth) is **Arm 4**.
4. **Box-loss restore (INV5, the engine's existing Tigris property, EBV3-9).** Drop the local CubDB → re-open the Volume → the archive reads identically; with `remote_cfg`, the engine lazily re-fetches `segments/{SEG}` from Tigris. The determinism posture (offline local-restore vs live-Tigris-tagged) is **Arm 5**.
5. **The two-app conformance/test posture (Arm 3).** A NEW store-side fold/restore/merge ExUnit suite gates the archive (the store has no `EchoMQ.Conformance`); IFF the optional bus-side `:archived` key lands (Arm 4), an additive `EchoMQ.Conformance` scenario (77 → 78, both pins re-pinned). Else the bus-side conformance is byte-frozen at 77.

All under the v2 master invariant (binding any BUS-SIDE surface UNCHANGED — the store-side engine code is governed by the BCS / engine laws, not the v2 wire grammar): braced `emq:{q}:` keyspace (any optional `:archived` key rides the existing `emq:{q}:stream:<name>:<sub>` form — NO grammar edit) · branded ids gated at the writer's door (emq3.2; the archive folds the writer's already-minted `EVT` ids, mints none) · every Lua key in `KEYS[]` or derived from a declared `KEYS[n]` root (emq3.5 adds NO new bus-side script — any `:archived` cache is a stock `SET`/`GET`, declared-keys VACUOUS) · the server clock where leases are touched (emq3.5 touches NO bus lease — the fold's ordering is enforced in the store-side consumer, not a `TIME` script) · inline `Script.new/2` (none added) · additive-minor conformance (77 byte-frozen, → 78 ONLY if the `:archived` key lands, Arm 4) · additive registration is a protocol MINOR, no wire break (`@wire_version` FROZEN `echomq:2.4.2`). **The shipped stream verbs / `EchoMQ.Stream` stay BYTE-FROZEN — the archive is purely additive and store-side.**

## Invariants (the runnable checks emq3.5 carries)

- **EMQ3.5-INV1 — fold-before-trim (the load-bearing no-loss invariant; F-2).** A record is trimmed from the live stream ONLY AFTER it is committed to the engine and `W` has advanced past it. *Check (POSITIVE, never a no-op):* the store-side fold suite appends K records, runs ONE fold-then-trim cycle, and asserts every trimmed record is READABLE from the archive AND the union archive ∪ live-tail == the original K (no record lost). The Director's mutation battery REORDERS the steps (trim-before-fold) and asserts the suite CATCHES the loss; a cycle that trims a record not yet folded is a LOUD failure. Because `XTRIM` returns only a count (F-2), the ordering is the ONLY defense — the consumer owns both calls.
- **EMQ3.5-INV2 — segment fold == stream slice (the order theorem extended to the archive).** The folded page payload's branded ids equal the trimmed slice's branded ids, in mint order. *Check:* fold a known K-record slice, enumerate the `@archive_base` range, and assert the recovered branded ids == the slice's branded ids in the SAME order (mint order); a reverse scan reads the archive newest-first, a forward scan oldest-first — both monotone (the order theorem, no second index).
- **EMQ3.5-INV3 — the merge-read property (archived ∪ live-tail, no gap / no overlap; F-1 / F-PLAT-B).** A read spanning the trim boundary returns the union, split on `W`; every record present once, none missing, none doubled. *Check:* append K, fold-then-trim a prefix (so `W` is mid-stream), merge-read the full range, assert EXACTLY the K records in mint order. No-gap/no-overlap is a CONSEQUENCE of INV1 + the order theorem (not a per-read check); the split point is `W` = the branded `EVT` id of the highest-folded record (F-1 — NOT the integer `head_lsn`).
- **EMQ3.5-INV4 — `@archive_base` disjoint from `@obx_base` (no page collision; F-LS-A).** No archive page index collides with an outbox-intent or business page index. *Check:* a unit assertion `@archive_base` is a high range disjoint from `@obx_base = :erlang.bsl(1, 48)`; a positive integration assertion that a Volume carrying BOTH outbox intents AND archive pages reads each range back correctly (the `idx >= @archive_base` filter vs the `@obx_base <= idx < @archive_base` filter). The mutation battery sets `@archive_base = @obx_base` and asserts the collision is CAUGHT.
- **EMQ3.5-INV5 — box-loss restore (drop local CubDB → restore → archive reads identical; EBV3-9).** *Check:* fold K records, DROP the local CubDB dir, re-open the Volume, assert the archive reads back identically (the offline local-restore path); the live-Tigris path (`:tigris`-tagged) re-fetches `segments/{SEG}` from a live bucket. The native engine's restore is unit-testable without live S3 (the `durability_test.exs` offline precedent); the determinism posture states which is in-suite and which is tagged (Arm 5).
- **EMQ3.5-INV6 — `W` is a branded `EVT` id read from the engine's frontier, NEVER `head_lsn` (F-1).** The merge-read split point `W` is the branded id of the highest-folded record (a CubDB frontier key the fold advances and/or the max id in the `@archive_base` range), comparable against live-tail ids via `Stream.Id.xadd_id/1`. *Check:* an assertion that the `W` reader returns a 14-byte branded `EVT` id (`EchoData.BrandedId.valid?/1` true, `Stream.Id.evt?/1` true), NOT an integer; that `W`'s `xadd_id` maps to the correct `XRANGE`/`XTRIM` bound; that the integer `head_lsn` is used ONLY to address pages, never as the merge split. A `W`-as-integer-LSN mutant (comparing an LSN against a branded id) is CAUGHT by the merge-read no-gap/no-overlap assertion.
- **EMQ3.5-INV7 — the bus-side surface is BYTE-FROZEN (the archive is additive + store-side).** The shipped stream verbs / `EchoMQ.Stream` (`append/4`/`append_id/5`/`append_batch/4`/`read/3..6`/`trim/4`/`stream_key/2`) + `EchoMQ.StreamRetention` + every shipped `@`-script are byte-identical to HEAD; `echo_wire` is UNTOUCHED; `@wire_version` reads `echomq:2.4.2`; `keyspace.ex` is unedited (any optional `:archived` key rides the existing `emq:{q}:stream:<name>:<sub>` form). *Check:* `git diff` of `stream.ex`/`stream_retention.ex`/`stream_consumer.ex`/`stream/id.ex` shows ONLY net-new additions IF ANY (the archive reads + trims via the existing public surface — ideally these files are EMPTY-diff and the only bus-side change is the optional `:archived` cache if Arm 4 lands); `grep -c redis.call` on the bus-side `lib/` diff = 0 (no new Lua); `git diff echo/apps/echo_wire/` EMPTY; `git diff keyspace.ex` EMPTY.
- **EMQ3.5-INV8 — the engine is COEXIST-canonical and UNTOUCHED (the native `EchoStore.Graft`; EBV3-2).** The fold targets the native engine via the PUBLIC `VolumeServer.commit/3` — it does NOT edit `volume_server.ex`/`store.ex`/`streamer.ex` (the engine's internals are frozen; the archive is a new CONSUMER of the public surface, the `EchoStore.Durability.Graft` precedent). The Rust `echo_graft_backend` is NOT touched (the coexisting peer, NOT the target). *Check:* `git diff` of the engine internals (`volume_server.ex`/`store.ex`/`reader.ex`/`streamer.ex`/`segment.ex`) is EMPTY (the archive lands as NEW store-side modules + the page-range landing, not engine edits); `echo_graft` (the Rust tree) is untouched; `mix.lock` unchanged (`cubdb` is already a declared `echo_store` dep — no new dependency).
- **EMQ3.5-INV9 — the two-app conformance/test posture (Arm 3).** The archive is gated by a NEW store-side fold/restore/merge ExUnit suite (INV1–INV6 as runnable checks); the bus-side conformance is byte-frozen at 77 UNLESS the optional `:archived` cache key lands (Arm 4 → 78, both pins re-pinned, the prior 77 byte-unchanged). *Check:* the new store-side suite exists and proves INV1–INV6 positively; IF the `:archived` key lands, `Conformance.run/2 → {:ok, 78}` with the prior 77 git-verified byte-unchanged + both pins re-pinned; IF deferred, `run/2 → {:ok, 77}` unchanged and the bus diff carries no conformance edit.

## Closed error set (the typed surfaces emq3.5 may meet — grounded, NO new typed bus-wire result)

- **`{:ok, lsn}` / `{:error, {:conflict, head}}` (the fold commit)** — `VolumeServer.commit/3`'s shipped returns (`volume_server.ex:50`): `{:ok, lsn}` on a clean OCC commit; `{:error, {:conflict, head}}` when the base snapshot is stale (a concurrent writer moved the head — the fold consumer retries upstream, re-`begin/1`-ing for the new head, the `EchoStore.Durability.Graft.record/4` retry precedent). NOT a new wire class — the engine's existing contract.
- **`{:ok, removed_count}` / `{:error, term}` (the trim, AFTER the fold)** — `EchoMQ.Stream.trim/4`'s shipped returns (`stream.ex:213`): the integer `XTRIM` answers; the trim runs ONLY after a successful fold + `W` advance (INV1). A trim `{:error, term}` AFTER a successful fold leaves the record SAFELY in the engine (over-retention, never loss — the safe direction); the next cycle re-trims (the trim is idempotent over the stream, the emq3.4 property). NOT a new wire class.
- **`{:ok, [{branded, fields_map}]}` / `{:error, term}` (the slice read + the merge-read)** — `EchoMQ.Stream.read/3..6`'s shipped returns (`stream.ex:270`): the slice in mint order, branded ids recovered from the stored `id` field; a `{:error, term}` (a wire fault) ABORTS the cycle BEFORE any trim (no fold, no trim — the safe direction). The merge-read returns the union in mint order or surfaces a store/wire fault verbatim.
- **`{:ok, branded_W}` / `:empty` (the archive frontier reader, forward-tense — F-1)** — the `W` reader: the branded `EVT` id of the highest-folded record, or `:empty` for a stream with nothing folded yet (the merge-read then reads the whole stream live — no archive seam). A 14-byte branded `EVT` id, NEVER an integer (INV6).
- **`:absent` (a page read past the archive frontier)** — `EchoStore.Graft.read_at/3`'s shipped `:absent` (`graft.ex`): a page index not in the snapshot. The archive enumerates ONLY the `@archive_base` range it folded; an out-of-range index is `:absent`, handled (the `replay/2` `else -> acc` precedent, `plugins/graft.ex:172`).
- **A malformed archived-stream declaration / a malformed window** — the fold consumer's config validation RAISES (or returns a typed `:invalid`) on a malformed archived-stream declaration at decision time (the `EchoMQ.StreamRetention.Core.resolve/2` gate-liveness precedent — a malformed policy is a configuration error, never a silent skip). A host-side contract, NOT a new wire class.

There is **NO new bus-side `EMQ*` wire class** — the archive folds via the engine's public surface + reads/trims via the shipped stream verbs; any optional `:archived` cache is a stock `SET`/`GET`. The closed registry stands `{EMQKIND, EMQSTALE}` ([`../../emq.design.md`](../../emq.design.md) §the closed wire-class registry). The engine's `{:conflict, head}` / `:absent` are EXISTING store-side contracts, not new wire classes.

## The testing posture (the determinism statement — Arm 5 decides the box-loss + live-Tigris cut)

emq3.5's HIGH risk is a DURABLE AT-REST ENGINE WRITE across a NEW cross-app surface with a NEW supervised process — three triggers (the §0 / D-2 derivation). The determinism posture:

- **The fold consumer is a PROCESS** (Arm 1 — supervised or host-wired) and the engine commit is an at-rest write — so the suite that exercises the fold-then-trim cycle + the merge-read is a candidate for the **≥100 determinism loop** (the program law's process/engine/at-rest-write trigger). BUT the fold consumes the writer's ALREADY-minted `EVT` ids (it mints none of its own — the same-millisecond mint hazard is absent in the FOLD itself); the hazard, if any, is in the test SETUP's `append/4` mints (which DO mint `EVT` ids). **The Director's verify settles the loop posture**: if the suite mints `EVT` ids in setup (it does, to build a slice), the ≥100 loop is RUN over the store-side fold suite (the same-ms mint hazard is live in the setup); the loop must OWN the machine (no concurrent liveness server). This is the HIGH-risk default — a process + an at-rest write + id-minting setup ⇒ the ≥100 loop, NOT a multi-seed sweep.
- **Box-loss restore (INV5) is unit-testable OFFLINE** (the `durability_test.exs` precedent — the engine's pure rollup/frontier/restore logic, no live S3): drop the local CubDB dir, re-open, assert identical reads. The LIVE-Tigris path is `:tigris`-tagged and run ONLY when a bucket is configured (the honest determinism posture states the cut — Arm 5).
- **The HIGH risk is absorbed by Apollo-mandatory + the Director's DEEPENED verify** (the ≥100 loop on the engine/process suite + the fold-then-trim mutation battery [reorder the steps; collide `@archive_base` with `@obx_base`; mutate the `W` split] + the two-app boundary grep + a box-loss adversarial probe), per the §0 / D-2 ruling.
