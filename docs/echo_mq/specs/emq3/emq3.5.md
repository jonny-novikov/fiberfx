## 0 · The slice — what emq3.5 builds, and why the archive is a store-side fold-then-trim

The tier ([`../../emq.streams.md`](../../emq.streams.md)) ships **event streams on the certified wire under the v2 laws, no second protocol**. Its three milestones are **S1 the writer** (emq3.1–3.2) → **S2 the readers** (emq3.3–3.4) → **S3 the memory** (emq3.5–3.6). emq3.5 is the FIRST rung of S3: the tier's stated **"deep history without resident memory"** need ([`../../emq.streams.md`](../../emq.streams.md) §The needs: *"Deep history without resident memory — stream segments folded to local CubDB and streamed to Tigris by the `EchoStore.Graft` engine (emq3.5)"*) and §The durable-archive answer (*"The archive tier (emq3.5) folds trimmed stream segments into the `EchoStore.Graft` engine's local CubDB and lets the engine stream those pages natively to Tigris S3 — one durable path, one knob (`remote_cfg`), not a sidecar … Box-loss recovery is the engine's lazy fetch of `segments/{SEG}` frames from Tigris — and the fold being a property of the engine already in place is what makes the archive nearly free"*).

**The never-deleted problem, and why retention alone is not the answer.** emq3.4 bounds a stream's memory by trimming entries past a declared window — a SAFETY (bounded memory) the operator declares per stream. But a trim is a PERMANENT removal: once `XTRIM` runs, the trimmed entries are gone (F-2). For a stream the operator wants bounded in RAM but DEEP in history (a recorded game's event log; an audit trail), retention alone loses the history. emq3.5 answers this: before a segment is trimmed, it is FOLDED durably into the native engine, where it survives box loss (the engine streams it to Tigris) and reads back beside the live tail. The trim stays bounded; the history stays whole. **Fold-before-trim is the load-bearing invariant** — it is the only ordering that loses no record (F-2): the consumer reads the slice, commits it, advances `W`, THEN trims.

**The archive lands store-side — the dependency law forces it (D-1, EBV3-3).** The engine (`EchoStore.Graft.*`) and the page store (`cubdb`) live in `echo/apps/echo_store`; `echo_mq` has NO `echo_store` dependency and NO `cubdb` (`echo_store/mix.exs:24-35` declares `echo_data` + `echo_mq` + `echo_wire` + `exqlite` + `cubdb`; the arrow is `echo_store → echo_mq`, one-way). The bus CANNOT call the store. So the fold consumer, the page-index landing, the merge-read, and the `W` reader all live store-side; at most an OPTIONAL `emq:{q}:stream:<name>:archived` polyglot cache key lands bus-side (a cache, never the source of truth — EBV3-5). **This is the FIRST stream rung to reach store-side** — the precision on the trim→fold→merge boundary is the whole rung. | Step | What happens | The surface (real or forward-tense) |
|---|---|---|
| **1 · read the about-to-expire slice** | `XRANGE` the stream's tail span that the retention policy is about to trim — recovering each record's branded `EVT` id from the stored `id` field, in mint order. | `EchoMQ.Stream.read/3..6` (`stream.ex:270`, SHIPPED), via `Driver.about_to_trim_slice/5` (`driver.ex:220`/`:234`, the `(W, floor]` bound — `from_bound/1` `:257` reads `(<W.xadd_id>` when a seam exists, else `-`; a `MINID` window reads strictly below the `minid_floor`, a `MAXLEN` window reads-all-above-`W`-then-drops-the-newest-`count`) → `{:ok, [{branded, fields_map}]}` in mint order. |

**INV1 — fold-before-trim (the load-bearing no-loss invariant).** A record is trimmed from the live stream ONLY AFTER it has been committed to the engine and `W` has advanced past it. *Check (POSITIVE, never a no-op):* the store-side fold suite appends K records to a stream, runs ONE fold-then-trim cycle, and asserts (a) every trimmed record is READABLE from the archive (the engine's `@archive_base` range, in mint order — segment fold == stream slice, INV2); (b) the live stream retains ONLY the un-folded tail; (c) the union of archive ∪ live-tail == the original K records, no gap / no overlap (INV3). A cycle that trims a record NOT yet in the engine is a LOUD failure — the Director's mutation battery REORDERS the steps (trim-before-fold) and asserts the suite catches the loss.

**Step ordering is the whole game (F-2).** Because `XTRIM` returns only a count (it cannot hand back what it deleted), the fold MUST precede the trim — there is no recovery after the trim. The consumer OWNS both calls (it reads the slice, folds, advances `W`, trims) precisely so the ordering is enforced in ONE process, not split across a bus event the store cannot observe (EBV3-3 — "no injected bus-side callback; the no-loss invariant must not live where the bus cannot verify it"). The trigger that STARTS a cycle — a tick, a depth watch, a hand-off from the emq3.4 driver — is **Arm 2** (the Director rules).

## 2 · The archive landing — a reserved high page range `@archive_base`, DISJOINT from any business page, the page payload carrying the branded id (INV4, F-LS-A / EBV3-1)

The fold commits each record as a CubDB page at a per-stream `@archive_base` range — a reserved high range on the native engine's flat page axis, far above any realistic business page:

1. **The engine's page axis is a flat `non_neg_integer()`** — `EchoStore.Graft.Store` keys pages as `{:page, page_idx, lsn}` (`store.ex:14`), `commit/3` stages a `%{page_idx => binary}` map (`volume_server.ex:50`), and a "business page" is simply whatever low index a workload writes (`0, 1, 2, …`); the engine reserves no range of its own. The archive claims a reserved HIGH range on this axis: `@archive_base = :erlang.bsl(1, 49)` (`stream_archive.ex:72`, AS-BUILT — pinned). `bsl(1, 49)` is far above any realistic business page count, so the archive range `[bsl(1, 49), ∞)` is DISJOINT from every business page by construction — NO archive page index can collide with a business page (far below `bsl(1, 49)`). The page axis is MULTIPLEXED (low business pages + the archive's high `@archive_base` range), GC indifferent to which range (the converged `local-store/cubdb` framing). **The disjointness is the invariant; the literal `bsl(1, 49)` is the settled constant.** *(D-5: the original reserved-range exemplar — the outbox adapter `@obx_base = :erlang.bsl(1, 48)` — is DROPPED this window (no backward compat); the archive owns the reserved-range mechanism natively against the engine's own page axis, so the disjointness is stated against a business page, not the vanished outbox range.)*
2. The page index is branded-id-MONOTONE: records fold in mint order (the order theorem extended to the archive — `EchoMQ.Stream.read/3..6` returns the slice in mint order, and the writer mints monotone `EVT` ids), so `@archive_base + n` for the n-th folded record is contiguous and ordered (the `Enum.with_index(base_n)` fold, `stream_archive.ex:121`). A forward scan of the `@archive_base` range reads the archive oldest-first, a reverse scan newest-first — NO second index (the order theorem, Appendix F extended to the log).
3. The page PAYLOAD carries the record's branded `EVT` id + its claims-only fields (`:erlang.term_to_binary({branded, fields})` — `stream_archive.ex:121`). The branded id IS the canonical receipt (a polyglot reader recovers it without re-encoding — the claims-only contract). Every record's id is asserted a well-formed `EVT` id before the fold (`assert_evt_slice!/1` `:277`, the kind discipline — a foreign brand in the archive would break the order theorem's "one namespace" premise).

**INV4 — `@archive_base` disjoint from every business page (no page collision).** No archive page index equals any business page index a workload writes through `commit/3`. *Check (AS-BUILT — recalibrated post-D-5, `stream_archive_test.exs:126`/`:136`):* a unit assertion `StreamArchive.archive_base() == :erlang.bsl(1, 49)` AND `> 1_000_000_000` (a reserved high range ~563 trillion indices above any realistic business page count — a forward `:arc_seq` allocator from 0 can never reach `2^49`, so `[2^49, ∞)` is disjoint from where real commits land by construction, `:126`); a positive integration assertion (`@tag :valkey`, `:136`) that a Volume carrying BOTH low-index business pages (staged at indices `0`/`1` via the PUBLIC `EchoStore.Graft.commit/3`, `:145`) AND archive pages (3 records via `fold/3`, `:150`) reads each range back un-corrupted — `read_archive/2` returns its 3 archive records (`:155`), `EchoStore.Graft.read(vol, 0)`/`read(vol, 1)` return the business pages un-corrupted (`:160`), `read(vol, 2) == :absent` (the archive's high pages absent from the low business range, `:163`), neither cross-contaminated. The Director's mutation battery sets `@archive_base` DOWN into the business range and asserts the collision is CAUGHT (an archive page overwrites a business page, or vice versa).

## 3 · The merge-read — over an engine-derived watermark `W` (a branded `EVT` id), no gap / no overlap (INV3, F-1 / F-PLAT-B / EBV3-5)

A deep read of an archived stream spans the trim boundary: records below the boundary live in the engine (the archive), records above it live on the wire (the live tail). The merge-read returns their UNION:

1. **`W` is the branded `EVT` id of the highest-folded record (F-1).** It is read from the engine's archive frontier — the CubDB key `:arc_frontier` the fold advances (`@frontier_key`, `stream_archive.ex:78`/`:254`) — NEVER the integer `head_lsn` (that is the engine's page cursor, the WRONG type). The `W` reader is `EchoStore.StreamArchive.archive_frontier/1` (`stream_archive.ex:144`, AS-BUILT) — `archive_frontier(db)` → `{:ok, branded_W} | :empty` (`db` the live CubDB `:db` pid `store_for/1` (`:223`) resolves from the engine's PUBLISHED read context `{:ctx, volume_id}` — the same lock-free context `EchoStore.Graft.Reader` reads, registered at `volume_server.ex:107` via `Map.take(state, [:volume_id, :db, …])` — NOT a phantom `{:store, vol}` registry name).
2. **The split is exact (the order theorem).** `merge_read/5` (`stream_archive.ex:191` — `merge_read(conn, queue, name, volume_id, db)`) splits on `W`: records with branded id ≤ `W` come from the engine's `@archive_base` range (`read_archive/2` `:161`); records with branded id > `W` come from the stream via `XRANGE` (`EchoMQ.Stream.read/6` with the lower bound `"(" <> W.xadd_id` — `w_bound/1` `:234`, exclusive; `<ms>` the real Unix-ms via `Stream.Id.xadd_id/1`). When `W` is `:empty` (nothing folded) the whole stream is read live (no archive seam). Because the fold is mint-ordered and fold-before-trim holds, `W` is precisely the seam: every record ≤ `W` is in the engine, every record > `W` is on the wire, and the two sets are DISJOINT and CONTIGUOUS (no gap, no overlap).
3. **No-gap/no-overlap is a CONSEQUENCE, not a check at read time (F-PLAT-B).** It follows from fold-before-trim (INV1 — nothing is trimmed before it is folded, so the live tail never has a gap below `W`) + the order theorem (the fold is mint-ordered, so `W` is a clean seam). The merge-read does NOT re-verify the boundary on every read; it trusts the invariant and splits on `W`.

**INV3 — the merge-read property: a read spanning the trim boundary returns archived ∪ live-tail, no gap / no overlap.** *Check:* the fold suite appends K records, folds-then-trims a prefix (so `W` is mid-stream), and a merge-read over the full range returns EXACTLY the K records in mint order — every record present once, none missing (no gap), none doubled (no overlap). A read of a range fully below `W` hits only the engine; a range fully above `W` hits only the wire; a range straddling `W` hits both and stitches at the seam.

- **A malformed archived-stream declaration / a malformed window** — the fold consumer's config validation RAISES (or returns a typed `:invalid`) on a malformed archived-stream declaration at decision time (the `EchoMQ.StreamRetention.Core.resolve/2` gate-liveness precedent — a malformed policy is a configuration error, never a silent skip). A host-side contract, NOT a new wire class.

There is **NO new bus-side `EMQ*` wire class** — the archive folds via the engine's public surface + reads/trims via the shipped stream verbs; any optional `:archived` cache is a stock `SET`/`GET`. The closed registry stands `{EMQKIND, EMQSTALE}` ([`../../emq.design.md`](../../emq.design.md) §the closed wire-class registry).
