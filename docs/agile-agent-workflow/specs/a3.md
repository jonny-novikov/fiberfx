# A3 · The roadmap layer — chapter spec

> The chapter spec for **A3 "The roadmap layer: Agile delivery & iteration"** — what the chapter delivers and proves,
> above its modules' stories. It is the agile-course analogue of a Portal chapter index (`phoenix/phoenix.md`): Goal,
> Rationale (the five Ws), Scope, Deliverables, Invariants, and a Definition of Done. The user stories are in
> [`a3.stories.md`](a3.stories.md); the build brief is [`a3.llms.md`](a3.llms.md); the plan is the single course
> roadmap [`aaw.roadmap.md`](aaw.roadmap.md).

**Parent:** [`aaw.roadmap.md`](aaw.roadmap.md) (the course) → **Part III**. Builds on **A2 · Decomposition**
(`/decomposition`, its value-ladder backlog is A3's input); feeds **A4 · The spec layer** (`/spec`, the specs a roadmap
line points at). **Children:** modules **A3.1–A3.9** (each a hub + ≥3 deep-dive subpages), plus the chapter landing and
three orientation dives.

## Goal

After A3, the reader can take a backlog — A2's dependency-ordered ladder of user stories — and **plan its delivery as a
`roadmap.md`**: thin, robust increments ordered into shippable milestones, run through an inspect-and-adapt loop, each
rung a line that points at a spec without restating it. The reader can read the real Portal `phoenix.roadmap.md` part
by part and write one for their own chapter.

## Rationale (5W)

- **Why** — A2 produces a backlog, but a backlog is not a plan. Without a delivery layer, work ships in the wrong order,
  or all at once (the big-bang failure A1.01 names). The roadmap layer is where *how we deliver* is decided, separately
  from *what we build* (A1.04), so the spec stays the single source of truth and the order can change under feedback.
- **What** — a teaching chapter on the roadmap layer: the agile values distilled (A3.1), XP for small batches (A3.2),
  the anatomy of a `roadmap.md` (A3.3), thin-but-robust (A3.4), milestones and iterations (A3.5), the program roadmap
  (A3.6), tracer bullets and walking skeletons (A3.7), a workshop (A3.8), and a reference appendix — glossary, sources,
  and the framework crosswalk (A3.9).
- **Who** — the **learner** (plans a delivery), the **developer** (roadmaps their own chapter), and the **Claude
  Author** (builds the modules from `a3.llms.md` + `a3.stories.md`).
- **When** — Part III, after Decomposition (A2) and before the spec layer (A4); the roadmap A3 teaches points forward at
  the specs A4 defines and the increments A5–A6 build.
- **Where** — served at `/course/agile-agent-workflow/roadmap`; specs under `docs/agile-agent-workflow/specs/`; the
  worked example is the Portal's real web chapter, F6 (`docs/elixir/specs/phoenix/phoenix.roadmap.md`).

## Scope

- **In:** the roadmap layer's concepts and artifacts, each landed on the real F6 exemplar; a UX-friendly reference
  appendix indexing the chapter/course terms to where the framework implements them.
- **Out:** the *content* of a spec (A4 — the spec layer), the agent brief and the build (A5), reliability (A6), and the
  end-to-end exemplar (A7). A3 plans delivery; it does not define behaviour or build it.

## Deliverables

- The chapter **landing** `/roadmap` (a recap + 5W overview) and **three orientation dives** (`where-we-are`,
  `the-roadmap-layer`, `the-road-ahead`).
- **Modules A3.1–A3.9**, each a hub + ≥3 deep-dive subpages with two interactives per lesson, grounded on the Portal.
- Every page's **md source of record** under `docs/agile-agent-workflow/content/roadmap/<module>/`.

## Invariants

- Every page lands a **principle → Portal practice** (the `.bridge`), reuses the design system without rebuilding it,
  and carries the segmented route-tag, the canonical footer, and real Sources links.
- Every `roadmap.md` example is **verbatim** from `phoenix.roadmap.md`; no rung, milestone, or Portal API is invented
  (only `Portal.ID` and the real F6 surfaces as they appear in `docs/elixir/specs/`).
- The roadmap **orders** work; it never defines behaviour — re-ordering rungs edits the roadmap, never a spec.

## Definition of Done

A module is done when **all ten `jonnify-cms` gates pass** (`STATUS: PASS`) on its hub and dives, the gate-invisible
checks hold (clamp spacing, segmented route-tag, real Sources, `node --check`, no invented API, no duplicate ids), the
relevant Given/When/Then in [`a3.stories.md`](a3.stories.md) are satisfied, and the **four living views agree** (the
served pages, the TOC, `course-map.md`, `llms.md`). The chapter is done when A3.1–A3.9 are all built and accepted.
