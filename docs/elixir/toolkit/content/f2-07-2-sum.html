Sum types

A sum type is one of several variants — one at a time, never together. Elixir spells it with tagged tuples: an atom in the first position names the variant, and the rest carries that variant’s data. The number of values a sum can hold is the sum of its variants’.

The tag is what makes the variants distinguishable. {:circle, r} and {:rectangle, w, h} are both tuples, but the leading :circle or :rectangle tells them apart at a glance and in a pattern match. The most common sum of all is {:ok, value} or {:error, reason}.

One of several shapes

A shape is a circle, a rectangle, or a triangle — each carrying different fields. The tag selects which, and a function can compute over all of them by matching the tag. Pick a variant.

Why they add

Because a value is exactly one variant, the totals do not multiply — they add. A result that is {:ok, a} or {:error, b} can take as many values as a has, plus as many as b has. A nullary variant like :pending contributes exactly one.

Set how many values each variant carries.

Idea

Mutually exclusive options add; a no-field variant adds one.

Elixir

This is why {:ok, _} | {:error, _} models “success or failure” exactly.

Variants add because only one holds at a time. Counting a sum is adding its variants’ values.
