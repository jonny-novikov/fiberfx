Design by contract

The contract triad Three deep dives

The walking skeleton runs, but its commands trust whatever they are handed. Design by contract makes that trust explicit. Every command carries three terms: a precondition the caller must satisfy to call it, a postcondition the command guarantees on success, and an invariant that is always true of the state, before and after. For enroll: the ids must be valid and the learner not already on the course; on success a fresh %Enrollment{} with progress: 0 is returned and stored; and progress never leaves 0..100. Elixir has no contract keywords, so these are written as guards, with chains, and tagged tuples — and they fail fast, at the boundary, before anything can be corrupted.

A contract wraps a command on three sides. Select a term to see what it says for enroll and who is responsible for it.

The contract triad

Read it left to right: the precondition is checked before the command runs, the postcondition holds when it returns, and the invariant constrains the state the whole time. The precondition protects the command from bad input; the postcondition lets the caller trust the result without re-checking; the invariant protects the entity from ever reaching an illegal shape. Select a term.

A contract turns assumptions into checks. Once the three terms are written down, “is this safe to call” has an answer in the code, not in your head.

Three deep dives

In order: the three terms of a contract and who owns each; how to express them in idiomatic Elixir; and why a contract must fail fast at the boundary rather than late and quietly.
