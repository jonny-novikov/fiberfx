**D-7 — Ledger edits never ride behind gates.** TOC and progress mutations run unconditionally, before any sweep, and print their effect; a failed gate blocks shipping, never bookkeeping. Adopted after sweep- and grep-gated chains silently swallowed the Appendix 1.1, Chapter 1.4, Appendix A, and Chapter 1.5 ledger updates (and note: `grep -c` exits nonzero on a zero count).

**D-8 — The registry grows by `ARC`.** Archetype definitions are entities: platform-scope namespace, minted and gated like any kind, registered by Chapter 2.4 under the bar that a kind earns a namespace when it needs identity and lifecycle of its own. The trading six remain as Part I reserved them.

**D-9 — The placement hash is validated where it serves and fenced where it fails.** One round stays the contract: trie windows measured clean, power-of-two moduli normed at k <= 8 on the committed octave-decay evidence, fairness constructed rather than hashed, and `hash32/v2` standing as the planned escalation for fan-out beyond the fence. **D-10 — The registry grows by `JOB`.** Work is a kind: minted, gated, browsed, audited, and one day replayed. Platform scope, registered by Chapter 3.2 under the D-8 bar. Refusals from bundle scripts lead with their own wire class (`EMQKIND` sets the pattern), and the enqueue script's act order — policy, existence, write — is normative for the bundle.
