The release timeline

From a first commit in 2011 to a stable release line shipping on a predictable cadence, Elixir has grown deliberately. Each milestone below adds one capability that shaped how the language is written today.

The cadence

Since v1.0 in 2014, Elixir has released a new minor version roughly every six months, around May and November. The early jump from a 2012 public release to a 2014 stable one reflects years of refining the core; since then the language has favoured small, steady additions over large rewrites. The most visible recent thread is the set-theoretic type system, which began as a preview and continues to grow.
